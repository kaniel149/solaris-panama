# Ideal Customer Profile — Solaris Panama

## Primary ICP: Commercial Rooftop Solar (Azuero Peninsula)

### Firmographics
- **Location:** Pedasi to Cañas corridor, Los Santos Province, Panama
- **Building type:** Commercial — hotels, resorts, supermarkets, agro-industrial, large fincas
- **Roof area:** 400 m²+ usable flat/low-slope roof
- **Electricity connection:** Active grid connection (ASEP net metering eligible)
- **Monthly electricity bill:** $1,500+ USD (ideal: $3,000+)
- **Decision-maker:** Owner-operator or on-site GM with budget authority

### Tier 1 — Highest Priority (Close first)
| Segment | Why | Examples |
|---------|-----|---------|
| **Hotels & Resorts** | High bills (AC 24/7), eco-branding ROI, owner-operated, visible roof | Villa Camilla, Casa Lajagua, Pedasi Sports Club |
| **Supermarkets** | Massive flat roofs, refrigeration = constant load, corporate ESG pressure | Super 99 Chitré, Super 99 Las Tablas |

### Tier 2 — Strong Fit
| Segment | Why | Examples |
|---------|-----|---------|
| **Large Fincas (agro)** | Galeras with big roofs, milking/processing = steady daytime load | Finca lechera Grade A, Finca El Espinal |
| **Surf camps / boutique lodges** | Eco-tourist clientele, marketing value, owner-run = fast decisions | Selina Playa Venao, Beach Break Surf Camp |

### Tier 3 — Opportunistic
| Segment | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Mini supers / small commercial** | Roof may be borderline 400m², but volume play if clustered |
| **New developments** | Pre-wire solar into construction — Cañas development land |

## Buying Signals (qualify fast)
1. Complains about electricity costs or outages
2. Already has generator (= pays premium for reliability)
3. Markets as "eco" or "sustainable" to tourists
4. Roof is flat metal/concrete with minimal obstructions
5. Expat owner (Canadian, American, European) — already familiar with solar
6. Expanding or renovating property

## Disqualification Criteria
- Roof < 300 m² usable
- No grid connection and no near-term plan for one
- Tenant with < 5 years remaining lease (unless landlord involved)
- Decision-maker unreachable after 3 attempts
- Property in legal dispute

## Decision-Maker Personas

### "The Expat Entrepreneur" (Hotels/Resorts)
- North American or European owner, lives on-site or visits frequently
- Speaks English, functional Spanish
- Thinks in USD, compares to home-country solar pricing
- Motivated by: ROI, property value, independence from grid
- Channel: WhatsApp (English) → Email with proposal

### "The Panamanian Chain Manager" (Supermarkets)
- Corporate structure, regional manager + HQ approval
- Speaks Spanish only, formal communication preferred
- Motivated by: cost reduction metrics, ESG compliance, competitor pressure
- Channel: Formal email (Spanish) → In-person meeting → Corporate proposal

### "The Finca Owner" (Agricultural)
- Panamanian family or investor, traditional business culture
- Relationship-first, needs trust before numbers
- Motivated by: reduce operating costs, power reliability, modernization
- Channel: Personal introduction → WhatsApp → On-site visit

## Key Insight
> In Azuero, **there are ZERO focused rooftop solar competitors**. Every prospect is a first-mover conversation, not a competitive displacement. The objection isn't "why you vs. competitor" — it's "why solar, why now."
