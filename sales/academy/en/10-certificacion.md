# Module 10: Simulations and Certification

> **Solaris Panama Sales Academy** | Final Module
> Estimated time: 4–6 hours (preparation + 8 role-plays + review with evaluator)

---

## What Is Solaris Certification?

The certification is not a multiple-choice exam. It is a practical demonstration that you know how to do the job.

To become certified, you must:
- Achieve an average of **≥80% on the quizzes** from modules 1 through 9.
- Pass **a minimum of 6 out of 8 role-plays** in this module with a score of ≥ 3/5.
- Complete **2 shadowing sessions** on real calls or visits with the manager or a certified salesperson.

Without certification, you do not conduct visits or send proposals without supervision.

---

## Structure of Each Scenario

Each role-play includes:
- **Setup:** context the salesperson knows before starting.
- **Prospect persona:** what the evaluator will portray (including hidden objections the salesperson doesn't know in advance).
- **Evaluation rubric:** 5 criteria, scale of 1–5.
- **Passing criterion:** average ≥ 3/5 on all criteria.

## Scenario 1: The Hot Residential Lead

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Olga Ortega, from Las Tablas, filled out the form yesterday. Bill: $200–$280 per month. Zinc roof. Wants to move forward "as soon as possible." They were sent the T+0 message and responded within 20 minutes: "Yes, with pleasure."

The salesperson must: conduct the 15-minute discovery call, qualify her, and close the call with one of the three valid outcomes (visit scheduled / proposal in 48h / friendly disqualification).

### Prospect Persona (only the evaluator knows this)
Olga is the property owner and has lived in the house for 8 years. The roof is about 90 m². She is ready, but her son lives with her and she wants him to be at the visit. If the salesperson doesn't ask who else participates in the decision, she won't mention it.

**Hidden objection:** She doesn't have the full capital. If the salesperson mentions financing or PPA before she asks, she is very interested. If it's not mentioned, she will say "I'll think about it."

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 (Poor) | 3 (Passing) | 5 (Excellent) |
|-----------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Rapport** | No greeting, jumps straight to questions | Greets, introduces themselves, warm tone | Personalizes the greeting with the Las Tablas detail, makes the prospect feel heard |
| **Discovery** | Asks fewer than 3 questions | Asks all 5 discovery questions | Asks all 5 AND follows up on the son detail (decision-maker) |
| **Objection handling** | Ignores the capital signal | Mentions PPA when it comes up | Detects the signal proactively and offers PPA, explains the model correctly |
| **Close** | Ends without proposing a next step | Proposes visit or proposal | Closes with a specific date and asks if the son can be present |
| **Professionalism** | Rushed tone, interrupts | Speaks clearly, respects pauses | Uses "usted," listens actively, tone of a reliable expert |

**Passing criterion:** Average ≥ 3/5. A 1 on "Discovery" is an automatic fail.

## Scenario 2: The Cold Call — Boutique Hotel (English Variant)

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Casa Lajagua is a boutique hotel owned by Canadian proprietors in Pedasi. They did not fill out any form. The salesperson will contact them for the first time via WhatsApp. The owner's name is Susan.

The salesperson must: make the first WhatsApp contact (text, evaluator reads the message as Susan), and if there is a positive response, conduct the 10-minute "call" introduction.

### Prospect Persona (only the evaluator knows this)
Susan is friendly but busy. She opens WhatsApp and if the message looks like spam or is too long, she doesn't respond. If the message is short, specific, and mentions something relevant to a hotel (AC costs, eco-branding), she responds.

During the "call," her main objection is: "We've thought about solar, but I'm not sure the roof can handle the weight of the panels."

**Note:** Susan prefers to converse in English. If the salesperson only speaks Spanish, they must say so naturally and offer to bring in someone who speaks English, or pass to the manager.

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **First WhatsApp message** | Long, generic, looks like spam | Short, mentions the hotel and electricity cost | Personalized: hotel name, specific pain point (AC, eco-branding), clear CTA |
| **Rapport (call)** | Ignores the language preference | Adapts to the language or explains the situation | Handles the language switch gracefully, confident tone |
| **Discovery** | Doesn't ask about roof or bill | Asks 3+ key details (bill, roof, occupancy) | Uncovers bill, roof type, usable area, and whether there is an additional decision-maker |
| **Objection (roof weight)** | Doesn't respond / gives incorrect information | Says they'll do a structural assessment on the visit | Briefly explains that the weight per m² is low and they always conduct an evaluation before installing |
| **Next step** | Ends without scheduling anything | Proposes a visit | Proposes a visit date with a roof evaluation included |

**Passing criterion:** Average ≥ 3/5. If the first WhatsApp message scores a 1, the scenario ends there (the evaluator does not "respond").

## Scenario 3: The Gatekeeper at the Supermarket

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
The salesperson arrives in person at Super 99 in Las Tablas to ask for the maintenance manager or the general manager. At the entrance there is a security guard and then a receptionist. The salesperson has no appointment and no contact name.

### Prospect Persona (the evaluator plays the guard and then the receptionist)
The guard says: "Do you have an appointment? The manager doesn't receive visitors without an appointment."
The receptionist (if they get that far) says: "Mr. Rodríguez is not available. I can let him know you came by."

**Hidden objection:** If the salesperson leaves a physical piece of material (business card, one-page flyer) and a concrete message ("Call me when you can, I have a savings analysis for your location"), the receptionist does write down the message. If they only leave their number verbally, she doesn't bother.

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Handling the guard** | Gets frustrated, insists, creates conflict | Accepts calmly and asks to speak with someone else | Treats the guard with respect, asks their name, explains the reason in one sentence |
| **Handling the receptionist** | Leaves only a verbal number | Leaves a business card and the name of the contact they're looking for | Leaves physical material + a specific message + asks for the manager's name and when a call would be appropriate |
| **Alternative proposal** | Proposes nothing | Says they'll call later | Asks for the management phone number to call themselves, or leaves a specific date ("I'll call Thursday at 9 AM") |
| **CRM logging** | Doesn't log the visit | Logs that they came | Logs: guard's name, receptionist's name, manager's name (if obtained), material left, next contact with date |
| **Professionalism** | Looks nervous, no materials | Has a business card, friendly tone | Appropriate attire, material in hand, explains the business in 15 seconds |

## Scenario 4: The Discovery Call with an Evasive Prospect

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Don Rafael, a dairy farm in La Villa, was referred by the owner of Villa Camilla. He has cattle and believes his bill is "about $1,000 per month" but has never actually checked it. He was contacted via WhatsApp and said "come by on Tuesday."

### Prospect Persona (only the evaluator knows this)
Don Rafael is friendly but vague. To every question he responds with "more or less" or "I don't know exactly." He doesn't have the bill. The shed roof is about 400 m² but he's not sure. He makes the decision on his own — no partner.

His real objection (he doesn't say it directly): he doesn't trust that the company is serious. People have come to visit him and promised things and then never followed up. If the salesperson shows a completed installation or gives the contact of a reference client in the area, the distrust drops significantly.

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Adapting to the pace** | Pushes for exact data, creates tension | Accepts vague answers and works with ranges | Works with ranges, takes notes, and proposes visiting the electrical installations together |
| **Creative discovery** | Only asks standard questions | Adapts questions to farm context | Asks about sheds, water pumps, milking, refrigeration — arrives at an estimated consumption without the bill |
| **Handling distrust** | Doesn't detect the signal | Detects that there's doubt and acknowledges it | Proactively offers a reference case in the area or proposes they speak with a current client |
| **Visit close** | Ends without a next step | Schedules technical visit | Schedules technical visit + gets Don Rafael's direct number + confirms he is the sole decision-maker |
| **Cultural respect** | Excessive formality or too informal | Friendly and respectful tone | Uses "usted," refers to the farm by the name Don Rafael uses, shows knowledge of the sector |

## Scenario 5: The Visit with the Skeptical Spouse

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Marco and Patricia Herrera, residence in Chitré, bill $320/month. Marco filled out the form, is convinced. They scheduled a visit for Saturday. When the salesperson arrives, Patricia is also present and says nothing at first.

### Prospect Persona
Marco: enthusiastic, asks technical questions, wants to know the kW and exact payback.
Patricia (secondary evaluator or evaluator who switches roles): silent skeptic. If the salesperson doesn't include her in the conversation, she will interrupt at the end with: "And what if the contractor disappears after installing it? Who guarantees the service?"

**Patricia's hidden objection:** She's not bothered by the cost. She's worried about post-sale reliability. If the salesperson mentions warranty, technical support, and gives a contact of a client who already has the system running, she gets on board.

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Inclusion of both** | Talks only to Marco, ignores Patricia | Includes Patricia in basic questions | Greets both individually, asks for Patricia's perspective, actively involves her |
| **Technical response (Marco)** | Gives vague or incorrect numbers | Gives estimated kW and payback with a range | Explains the right system, shows the calculation, covers Law 417 and net metering with ASEP |
| **Handling Patricia's objection** | Gets defensive, seems offended | Explains system warranty | Provides warranty, mentions post-sale support, offers a reference client contact in the area |
| **Close** | Ends without commitment from both | Proposes formal proposal | Gets agreement from BOTH before leaving, not just Marco |
| **Visit management** | Disorganized visit | Reviews roof and obtains technical data | Reviews roof, takes measurements or photos, explains the full installation and ASEP process |

## Scenario 6: The Price Objection Storm

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Héctor, small business in Pedasi. Bill $450/month. Proposal sent 3 days ago: 8 kW system, $10,500 USD. He responded via WhatsApp: "Hey, that's really expensive. Can't you lower it?" The salesperson calls him to talk.

### Prospect Persona
Héctor will launch 4 consecutive price objections. If the salesperson resolves one, he launches the next. The 4 objections:
1. "It's much more expensive than I expected."
2. "A neighbor told me there are Chinese panels at half the price."
3. "I don't have that capital right now."
4. "Can't you give me a discount?"

If the salesperson resolves all 4 without lowering the price and without making promises they can't keep, Héctor says: "Okay, let me think about it with my wife."

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Objection 1 (price high)** | Justifies or promises to lower | Reframes with ROI: "$10,500 vs. $5,400/year in bills" | Calculates total return: "In 4 years it pays for itself, then 20 years of nearly free electricity" |
| **Objection 2 (Chinese panels)** | Speaks badly about the competition | Differentiates on warranty and durability | Differentiates specifically: panel warranty, inverter warranty, local support, reputation in Azuero |
| **Objection 3 (no capital)** | Lowers the price | Presents PPA ($0 upfront, pays per kWh) | Presents PPA, calculates month-1 savings compared to current bill |
| **Objection 4 (discount)** | Gives a discount without authorization | Says they need to check with the manager | Explains the price includes installation, ASEP paperwork, warranty — and asks what aspect of the cost concerns them most |
| **Transition to "let me think about it"** | Pushes to close now | Accepts and proposes follow-up | Accepts calmly, asks what specific doubt they'll bring to the conversation with their wife, and offers material so she can also review it |

**Note to evaluator:** If the salesperson lowers the price without manager authorization at any of the 4 moments, the scenario is an automatic fail.

## Scenario 7: "Let Me Think About It" at the Moment of Close

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Carmen, residence in Los Santos. Bill $190/month. 6 kW proposal, $7,800. The salesperson is on the second follow-up call. Carmen has been "thinking about it" for 12 days. This call is the moment to attempt the final close.

### Prospect Persona
Carmen is actually convinced, but she is afraid of making the wrong decision. Her real objection is: "What if something goes wrong with the installation?" She doesn't say it directly. If the salesperson asks specifically "Is there a specific doubt that's generating hesitation for you?", she admits it.

If the salesperson tries to close without exploring the specific doubt, Carmen says "let me think about it" and hangs up. If they explore the doubt and resolve it, Carmen says: "Okay, how do we proceed?"

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Detection of the real hesitation** | Doesn't ask, assumes it's about the price | Asks if there is any outstanding doubt | Asks specifically: "Is there something specific generating hesitation — the process, the results, the company?" |
| **Resolution of the doubt** | Doesn't resolve it or gives a generic response | Gives a response about warranty / process | Gives a concrete answer about what happens if something fails (warranty, support contact, claims process) |
| **Closing technique** | Directly pushes for the signature | Proposes moving forward in small stages | Uses cascade commitment: "How about we schedule the technical visit with no installation commitment yet?" |
| **Handling "let me think about it"** | Insists or gets frustrated | Respects and proposes a follow-up with a date | Respects, asks when they might have an answer, and schedules the next call |
| **Professionalism** | Anxious or impatient tone | Calm tone | Confident tone, no rush, makes Carmen feel the decision is hers |

## Scenario 8: The Referral Request

### Setup (the salesperson knows this)
Don Ernesto just signed a contract for a 10 kW system at his business in Chitré. The salesperson calls to confirm next steps (technical coordination). It is the moment to ask for referrals.

### Prospect Persona
Don Ernesto is happy with the decision but will not volunteer referrals on his own. If the salesperson doesn't ask, he doesn't give them. If the salesperson asks vaguely ("do you know anyone?"), Don Ernesto says "yes, sure, if I think of someone I'll let you know."

If the salesperson asks for a referral specifically ("is there someone in particular you think would benefit — a neighbor, a business partner?") and then says "with your permission I'll contact them, I just need the number," Don Ernesto gives 2 names.

### Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Timing of the ask** | Doesn't ask for referrals | Asks at the end of the call | Asks after confirming next steps, at the moment of highest client enthusiasm |
| **Specificity of the ask** | Vague question: "Do you know anyone?" | Asks about neighbors or partners | Asks about a specific person in mind: similar business, nearby area, electricity complaint |
| **Referral close** | Asks Don Ernesto to call them | Asks for the name | Asks for name + number and says "with your permission I'll contact them on your behalf" |
| **Incentive** | Doesn't mention the benefit | Mentions the credit [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE] | Mentions the credit and briefly explains it ("if they close, you receive...") |
| **CRM logging** | Doesn't log the referrals | Notes down the names | Notes name, number, relationship to Don Ernesto, and creates the leads in the CRM immediately |

## Final Certification Criteria

To earn the Solaris Panama certification:

### Requirement 1: Quizzes
- Average quiz score across modules 1 through 9: **≥ 80%**
- You cannot have any module with a failed quiz (< 60%) without having retaken it.

### Requirement 2: Role-Plays
- Pass a minimum of **6 out of 8 scenarios** with an average ≥ 3/5.
- Failed scenarios may be repeated **once** after practice.
- An automatic fail (lowering price without authorization, first WhatsApp ignored) cannot be recovered in the same session — it requires 24 hours of practice.

### Requirement 3: Shadowing Sessions
- Complete **2 shadowing sessions** on real calls or visits:
  - Shadowing 1: silent observer (the senior salesperson speaks, the candidate observes and takes notes).
  - Shadowing 2: the candidate speaks, the senior salesperson observes and gives immediate post-call feedback.

### Certification
Upon meeting all 3 requirements, the manager signs the certification and the salesperson is authorized to:
- Conduct visits without supervision.
- Send proposals (with manager review before sending).
- Request referrals on behalf of Solaris.

The certification is renewed with a performance review every 6 months.

---

## Notes for the Evaluator

- Role-plays are conducted in sessions of no more than 3 scenarios in a row — the candidate needs time to process feedback.
- After each scenario, the evaluator gives 3 minutes of verbal feedback: what worked, what to change, what to practice.
- If the candidate shows high anxiety, consider running an ungraded "warm-up scenario" before starting.
- The evaluator must not interrupt during the role-play — only at the end.
- Record all notes in writing for the 6-month performance review.
