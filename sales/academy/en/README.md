# Solaris Panama Sales Academy

> For new salespeople beginning their solar career in Azuero.
> This academy takes someone with zero solar experience and turns them into a certified salesperson, ready to run site visits and send proposals.

---

## Why Does This Academy Exist?

Solaris Panama operates in the Azuero Peninsula — a region with no direct competition in commercial and residential solar. That means every conversation with a prospect is not "why Solaris instead of someone else?" but "why solar, why now?"

That conversation requires confidence, product knowledge, and respect for the local culture. This academy delivers all three.

## The Solaris Salesperson's Daily Routine

A typical day after certification:

**Morning (8:00 - 10:00 AM — optimal contact window)**
- Review the CRM: which leads have follow-ups due today?
- Send pending follow-up messages (T+48h, T+5d, T+10d).
- Make scheduled discovery calls.

**Mid-morning (10:00 AM - 12:00 PM)**
- Prepare proposals for ready prospects.
- Coordinate technical visits.
- Update the CRM with every contact made during the day.

**Afternoon (2:00 - 4:00 PM — second optimal window)**
- In-person visits (always confirmed 24 hours in advance).
- Cold prospecting if no visits scheduled (WhatsApp to hotels, supermarkets, farms).
- Follow-up calls to deals that didn't respond in the morning.

**End of day (4:00 - 5:00 PM)**
- Verify that all contacts made during the day are logged in the CRM.
- Confirm that no deal is left without a next action.
- Prepare the action list for the following day.

**Friday (weekly close)**
- Weekly report to the manager: calls, visits, proposals, closes, reactivated leads.
- Pipeline review with the manager: what advanced, what is stalled, what needs support.

## Solaris Panama's Golden Rules

1. **Nothing goes out without the manager's approval.** Proposals, special prices, PPA terms — everything goes through the manager before it reaches the client.

2. **WhatsApp is the primary channel.** Do not cold call without having sent a WhatsApp first. Do not send an email without having tried WhatsApp.

3. **Always "usted."** Using informal address in B2B sales in Panama is discourteous. Always "usted."

4. **Best hours:** Tuesday through Thursday, 8-10 AM and 2-4 PM Panama time. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.

5. **In Azuero, everyone knows everyone.** Your reputation is your most valuable asset. How you end a lost deal matters as much as how you start it.

6. **The CRM is your memory.** If it's not logged, it doesn't exist.

## Frequently Asked Questions — New Salesperson

**What do I do if a prospect asks me something technical I can't answer?**
Don't make something up. Say: "Great question — let me confirm that with the technical team and I'll get back to you tomorrow." Write it down and check with the manager. "I don't know, but I'll find out" builds more trust than a wrong answer.

**Can I give a price over WhatsApp before the visit?**
General ranges only (for example, "residential systems of 5-7 kW are between $6,000 and $9,000 depending on the roof"). Never a firm price before visiting the property. Roofs vary — a price given without seeing the roof commits the company.

**What do I do if the prospect wants to speak in English and my English isn't strong?**
Be honest: "My English is basic — would it be okay to continue in Spanish, or would you prefer I connect you with our manager who speaks English?" Don't fake a language you don't have a strong command of. Losing credibility over language is worse than acknowledging the limitation.

**How long should I wait between T+5d and T+10d if the prospect responds something vague like "I'm thinking about it"?**
If they respond "I'm thinking about it," the follow-up clock resets. Respond with a specific question ("Is there a particular concern that's creating hesitation?") and wait for their response. Don't jump to T+10d until the prospect is truly in complete silence.

**Can I do visits on weekends?**
Only if the prospect explicitly requests it. In general, Saturday mornings are acceptable for residential properties. Sundays are for family in Azuero — never contact on a Sunday unless it's a client emergency.

**What do I do if during the visit the roof won't work?**
Be direct but kind: "Looking at the roof, I can see that [shade / insufficient area / angle] complicates the installation. I don't want to propose something that won't perform well. Do you have another property or available area?" If there's no viable solution, close gracefully, document in the CRM, and ask for referrals before you leave.

**Does PPA apply to all clients?**
No. PPA requires specific conditions that the manager evaluates on a case-by-case basis (minimum system size, contract length, client billing history). Never promise PPA without the manager confirming it. You can mention it as an existing option, but not as a guaranteed offer.

**When can I send a proposal on my own, without the manager reviewing it?**
Until you earn certification: never. After certification: the manager reviews before sending, but the goal is to reduce that review time to under 4 hours. As you gain experience and the manager trusts your judgment, the process speeds up.

## The Azuero Context — What You Need to Know Before You Start

Azuero is not Panama City. It is a region with its own culture, where business is done between people who have known each other all their lives.

**Decision pace:** In the capital, deals close fast. In Azuero, trust comes before numbers. A prospect who has known you for 3 weeks and gives you a referral is worth more than 10 leads from a Google ad.

**The power of referrals:** In Las Tablas, if you install solar on the most visible hotel in town, the hardware store owner across the street will call you in less than a month. The visibility of installations is free advertising. Each system installed is a billboard that works for 20 years.

**Law 417:** It is real and in effect — it exempts panels, inverters and solar equipment from import taxes. Use it honestly as an urgency argument, without exaggerating. Don't promise it will last forever, because laws change. The right message is: "While the law is in effect, it makes sense to take advantage of it."

**ASEP and net metering:** ASEP is Panama's National Authority for Public Services. Net metering allows you to feed surplus energy back into the grid and deduct it from your bill. The process takes weeks — Solaris handles it on behalf of the client, but the client should know a process exists. Don't sell solar as if it were instantaneous.

**Seasons:** The dry season (December to April) is the best time to install — less rain, fewer construction delays. The rainy season doesn't prevent installation, but it complicates logistics. Plan your closing conversations with this in mind: if you close in July, installation will likely happen in December.
