# Module 6: The Visit — From Inspection to Personalized ROI

> **Level:** Beginner | **Estimated time:** 90 minutes | **Prerequisite:** Modules 1–5

---

## 🎯 Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Arrive at every visit completely prepared — with the prospect's information, the right materials, and numbers ready.
2. Open the first 5 minutes by building trust, without sounding like a salesperson.
3. Walk the roof with basic judgment and make useful estimates.
4. Present the personalized ROI following the Solaris pitch deck flow.
5. Read the prospect's signals to know when to advance and when to listen.
6. Close the visit with a concrete next step — never leave without one.

---

## Theory: The Visit Is Where Deals Are Won

The phone opens the door. The visit closes the deal.

In the business culture of Azuero, no email, video, or brochure can replace a person who is physically present, understands your business, walks your roof, and speaks your numbers. The visit is your most powerful moment — prepare it accordingly.

**The primary goal of the visit is not to close the sale that day** (though it can happen). The goal is to advance the process with a concrete next step: formal proposal, meeting with the partner, contract signature.

---

## Before You Arrive: Total Preparation

The visit is won or lost before you get there.

### What You Must Bring

- [ ] Tablet or laptop with the pitch deck loaded — no Wi-Fi dependency
- [ ] ROI calculator ready (the pricing model formula — see below)
- [ ] Prospect's electricity bills if shared, or ask them to have them available
- [ ] Solaris Panama business cards
- [ ] Measuring tape for roof estimates
- [ ] Phone for roof photos — full angles
- [ ] Notebook for notes: prospects notice when you write down what they say

### What You Must Know Before Arriving

- [ ] Contact's full name and actual role (are they the owner? manager? person in charge who needs authorization?)
- [ ] Business type: hotel, farm, retail, agricultural operation
- [ ] Approximate monthly electricity bill (if shared during the call)
- [ ] Estimated roof area (check Google Maps Satellite if possible)
- [ ] Whether they've already spoken to another solar company (yes/no — important for your positioning)
- [ ] Conversation history: what motivated them to contact us?

### Mental Preparation

Before you walk in, remember: your job is not to convince — it's to diagnose and show. If the ROI makes sense for this prospect, the numbers do the convincing on their own. If it doesn't make sense, tell them honestly.

## The First 5 Minutes: Trust Before Solar

In Panama, the relationship comes before the business. A prospect who doesn't trust you as a person won't buy, no matter how compelling the ROI.

### The Arrival Protocol

**Rule:** Arrive 5–10 minutes early. Never late. Punctuality communicates respect in Panamanian business culture.

When greeting:
- Firm handshake, eye contact, genuine smile.
- Use **"usted"** until the prospect switches to "tú" — never the other way around.
- If other people are present (partner, administrator, spouse), greet everyone with equal respect. Any one of them could be the real decision-maker.

### The Opening: Don't Talk About Solar Yet

The first 2–3 minutes are for connecting as people.

Natural opening questions:
- "How long has this business been here?" — invites the prospect to talk about what matters most to them.
- "Are you from the region or did you come to settle here?" — many owners in Azuero have deep local roots.
- "How has the season been?" — for hotels and farms, this is always relevant.

**Listen genuinely.** The details they share now will serve you during the presentation.

### The Bridge to the Topic

Once the prospect has spoken for 2–3 minutes, transition naturally:

> "Don [NOMBRE], and how has the electric bill been? Because that's exactly what we want to look at with you today."

## The Roof Inspection

Before presenting any numbers, see the roof. Two reasons:
1. The ROI numbers must be real — you can't guess.
2. The roof visit is experiential: the prospect visualizes the system in their actual space.

### What to Observe

- **Total available area** (use the measuring tape or estimate visually)
- **Orientation:** ideally facing south or south-west in Panama
- **Shade:** trees, structures, tanks, chimneys — shade reduces production
- **Material:** metal sheet, concrete, tile — affects installation type
- **General condition:** are there leaks, rust, or visible structural damage?
- **Access:** is there safe space for installation work?

### How to Estimate the System in the Field

Pricing model formula, applied on the roof:

```
Usable roof (m²) = Total area × 0.65   [conservative factor]
System size (kW) = Usable roof × 0.15
```

Quick example on a visit:
- 800 m² roof → 520 m² usable → system of ~78 kW → we round to 80 kW

Take photos from every angle. The technical team needs them for the final design.

> ⚠️ **Never guarantee exact numbers on the roof.** Always say: "This is an initial estimate — the technical team confirms the numbers before any formal proposal."

## Presenting the Personalized ROI: The Pitch Deck Flow

After the inspection, sit the prospect down and present. Follow this order — each step prepares the prospect for the next.

### Step 1 — The Problem (start with the pain)

> "Don [NOMBRE], you told me you pay $[MONTO_FACTURA] per month in electricity. That's not a coincidence — hotels and businesses in Azuero are paying between $3,000 and $8,000 monthly. And electricity rates in Panama have risen more than 30% in the last 5 years. That's not going to stop."

### Step 2 — The Opportunity

> "But there's something most property owners don't see: your roof is already capturing energy — it's just being wasted. Panama receives between 5 and 6 kWh of solar radiation per square meter per day. That's world-class. Your roof is an untapped power plant."

### Step 3 — Law 417

> "And this is what makes Panama special: the government created Law 417 exactly for this. Tax exemption on equipment and installation, duty-free import on all solar components, and net metering — whatever you produce in excess, the utility credits back to you. It applies to systems up to 500 kW, which covers any commercial building."

### Step 4 — What Solaris Does

> "We handle absolutely everything: the roof evaluation — which we're doing today —, the custom design for your actual consumption, the installation with all permits, the paperwork with ASEP, and system monitoring with a 25-year panel warranty."

### Step 5 — The ROI (the key moment)

Use the prospect's numbers here. Calculate in front of them:

> "Now your numbers. With a roof of approximately [ÁREA_M2] m², we're looking at a system of around [TAMAÑO_KW] kW. That produces about [PRODUCCIÓN_KWH] kWh per month. At $0.20 per kWh — the average commercial rate in Panama — that's $[AHORRO_MENSUAL] in monthly savings."

> "The system costs approximately $[COSTO_SISTEMA]. With the Law 417 benefits, the real payback period is 2.5 to 3.5 years. After that: essentially free electricity for 20 more years."

> ⚠️ Always say "approximately" — the exact price is confirmed by management with the final technical design.

### Step 6 — Payment Options

Always present in this order — start with direct purchase to anchor maximum value:

**1. Direct purchase:** "You pay once and it's yours forever. The highest ROI."

**2. PPA (Power Purchase Agreement):** "Zero upfront. We install the system, you buy the energy it produces at a rate of $0.14–$0.17 per kWh — less than what you pay today. Savings from month one."

> "Think of it as switching electricity providers — but one that charges you less and lives on your own roof."

**3. Lease:** "Fixed monthly payment, predictable, lower than your current bill. No surprises."

### Step 7 — Why Solaris

> "We are the only company focused exclusively on commercial rooftop solar in Azuero. We're not based in Panama City sending technicians when there's a problem. We're here, in the region. When something happens, we show up."

If you have a local reference, use it:
> "We're working with properties in [REFERENCIA_LOCAL] — you can ask their neighbors directly."

## Reading the Room

During the presentation, observe.

### Signals of Genuine Interest
- Asks specific questions: "How long does the installation take?"
- Calls their partner or spouse: "Hold on, let me get [NOMBRE]"
- Takes photos of the ROI with their phone
- Calculates on their own: "So in about 4 years I've already recovered it..."
- Speaks in the future tense: "And how does the monitoring work once it's installed?"

### Signals of Caution (there is an unspoken doubt)
- Polite but short answers: "Yes, that's interesting."
- Crosses arms when you mention the system cost
- Checks their phone frequently
- Says "let me think about it" without having asked any questions

**When you see caution, do not accelerate.** Ask an open question:
> "Don [NOMBRE], is there anything we've looked at that raises a question for you? I'd rather address it now."

## Closing the Visit: Never Without a Next Step

The visit always ends with a clear agreement on what happens next. Without it, the prospect "cools off" and follow-up becomes complicated.

### Options Based on the Moment

| Prospect's situation | Proposed next step |
|---|---|
| Enthusiastic, few doubts | "Should we get the formal proposal started this week?" |
| Needs to consult with partner/spouse | "When do you meet? I can be available to answer questions." |
| Wants to compare with another company | "Perfect. Shall we talk on [DÍA] to go through it point by point?" |
| Needs more time | "What additional information would give you more confidence?" |

**Always offer two specific dates.** Do not ask "when is good for you?" — that is too open and does not generate commitment.

## 📞 Visit Scripts

### Upon Arrival

> "Don [NOMBRE], great to meet you — [YOUR NAME] from Solaris Panama. Thank you for having me. How long has this hotel been here in [LUGAR]?"

### Transition to the Roof

> "Perfect, Don [NOMBRE]. When you're ready, I'd like to take a look at the roof so I have the real numbers. How do we access it?"

### After the Inspection, Before Presenting

> "Very good. I saw excellent conditions. With what we have, I can already show you what this means for your business in concrete numbers. Shall we sit down for a moment?"

### When Presenting the ROI

> "Don [NOMBRE], based on what you told me — that you pay $[MONTO_FACTURA] per month — and the roof I just saw, here is approximately what your business could generate: [PRESENT THE ROI]. Does that make sense to you?"

### Visit Close — Prospect Ready

> "Don [NOMBRE], with what we saw today, I can have a formal proposal with exact numbers ready in [X DAYS]. Would it work to meet on [DÍA] at [HORA] to review it together?"

### Visit Close — Prospect Needs Time

> "Completely understandable. What specific information would help you feel confident about this decision? I'll prepare exactly that for our next conversation."

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Talking about solar before connecting as a person.** Relationship first, then numbers.
2. **Using generic numbers without calculating the prospect's ROI.** Personalized numbers are the only ones that truly persuade.
3. **Guaranteeing exact results on the roof.** Always "approximately" — the technician confirms.
4. **Leaving without a concrete next step.** A visit without a follow-up date is time invested with no result.
5. **Talking more than you listen.** In Azuero culture, whoever listens more, sells more.
6. **Going straight to price without presenting value first.** Price without value = immediate objection.
7. **Ignoring other people present at the meeting.** The partner or spouse may be the real decision-maker.

## 🎭 Role-Play: The Complete Visit

**Situation:** You are visiting Don Ricardo, owner of a 20-room hotel in Chitré. He pays $3,800/month in electricity. The roof measures approximately 700 m² in total.

**Partner A — Don Ricardo:** Be friendly but skeptical. When you see the system cost say: "That's a lot of money." When they try to close, say: "Let me think about it."

**Partner B — Solaris Salesperson:** Complete all 6 steps of the visit.

**Salesperson tasks:**
1. Open the conversation without mentioning solar for the first 2 minutes.
2. Visit the roof (simulate standing up) and estimate the system using the formula.
3. Present the 7 pitch deck slides with Don Ricardo's numbers.
4. When he says "that's a lot of money" — apply what you learned in Module 7.
5. Close with a concrete next step with date and time.

**Time:** 15 minutes per round. Then switch roles and discuss what worked.
