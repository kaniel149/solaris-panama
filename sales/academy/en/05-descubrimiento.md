# Module 5: Discovery — The Call That Decides Whether There's a Deal

> **Level:** Beginner | **Estimated time:** 75 minutes | **Prerequisite:** Modules 1–4

---

## 🎯 Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Conduct the 15-minute discovery call with a clear structure
2. Ask the 5 qualifying questions in the correct order
3. Request the electric bill photo via WhatsApp during or after the call
4. Identify buying signals and disqualification criteria
5. Close the call with a site visit scheduled — date and time confirmed

---

## Theory: What Is the Discovery Call For?

The discovery call **is not a sales pitch.** It is a 15-minute interview where you diagnose whether this customer is a strong solar candidate and whether we are the right solution for them.

**The principle:** Diagnose before prescribing. A doctor doesn't write a prescription without first examining the patient. You don't propose a system without first understanding the property, the consumption, and the decision-maker.

### The Three Possible Outcomes

At the end of the 15-minute call, you must come away with ONE of these three outcomes:

| Outcome | What it means | Next step |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **(A) Site visit scheduled** | The customer qualifies and there is genuine interest | Visit confirmed with date and time |
| **(B) Direct proposal** | They already have roof and bill photos — no visit needed | Proposal ready within 48 hours |
| **(C) Friendly disqualification** | Does not qualify — bill too low, tenant without owner, unviable roof | Honest close + ask for referrals |

If the call ends without any of these three outcomes — "I'll think about it and let you know" — you did not close the call. Return to the closing script.

### Call Structure — 15 Minutes

| Minutes | Phase | Objective |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| 0:00 – 1:00 | Opening and confirmation | Confirm name, provide context, ask permission to ask questions |
| 1:00 – 8:00 | The 5 qualifying questions | Bill, property, roof, decision, budget |
| 8:00 – 11:00 | Summary and diagnosis | Repeat what you heard, validate that solar makes sense |
| 11:00 – 14:00 | Close — schedule next step | Propose visit or proposal with a specific date |
| 14:00 – 15:00 | Confirmation and close | Confirm date, send WhatsApp reminder |

## 📞 Scripts

### Call Opening (Minutes 0–1)

---

> "Good [MORNING/AFTERNOON] [NOMBRE], this is [AGENTE] with Solaris Panama. Thank you so much for taking the time — I promised 15 minutes and I'll keep to that.
>
> To make the most of your time, I'd like to ask you 5 quick questions about your property and energy usage. With that information I can tell you today whether solar makes sense for you and what size system you'd need. Does that work for you?"

---

### The 5 Qualifying Questions (Minutes 1–8)

Ask the questions in this exact order. Do not skip any.

---

**Question 1 — The Bill (qualifier #1)**

> "To confirm — how much did you pay on your last electricity bill? Do you have the bill handy?"

**Why it's first:** The bill is the most important qualifier. If the amount is too low, solar won't provide a reasonable return and you must disqualify honestly. If it's high, there's a deal.

**Green signal:** Residential bill of $150/month or more. Commercial bill of $1,500/month or more.
**Yellow signal:** Residential bill of $80–$150/month — explore PPA.
**Red signal:** Residential bill under $80/month — disqualify respectfully.

**After they answer:**

> "Would you mind taking a photo of that bill and sending it to me on WhatsApp when we finish the call? With that exact number I can prepare a personalized analysis for you."

---

**Question 2 — The Property**

> "Is the property yours or do you rent? How long have you been there?"

**Why it matters:** A tenant without the owner involved cannot sign a contract or authorize installation. If they rent with fewer than 5 years left on the lease and the owner isn't part of the conversation, we disqualify.

**If they rent:**

> "Do you have access to the owner? To install, we would need their authorization — they benefit too, since it increases the property value."

---

**Question 3 — The Roof**

> "Is the roof zinc, concrete, or tile? Do you have a rough idea of the square footage?"

**Why it matters:** We need to know whether the roof can support panels and whether it has enough usable area. Residential minimum 30 m², commercial minimum 300–400 m².

**If they don't know the size:**

> "No problem — we measure it on the site visit. Just a rough idea: could you park a car on the roof, or is it small?"

**If they mention shade from trees or buildings:**

> "Is the shade there all day or only at certain times? We evaluate that on the visit with a solar analysis tool."

---

**Question 4 — The Decision-Maker**

> "If the numbers work out, who else would be involved in the decision — spouse, business partner, family?"

**Why it matters:** If there is another person who must approve the purchase and they are not on the call, you cannot move to a proposal without including them. Better to know now.

**If there is another person involved:**

> "Understood. Would you like both of you to be at the next meeting? That way you don't have to relay information back and forth — we talk together and answer all questions on the spot."

---

**Question 5 — Budget and Model**

> "Do you have an investment range in mind, or would you prefer to explore a no-upfront-cost option — the PPA model where you put nothing down?"

**Why it matters:** Determines whether we present direct purchase (best ROI) or PPA (no upfront investment). Both options are valid.

**If they say they have no budget:**

> "Then the PPA may be perfect — you put in nothing, we install the system, and you pay for the energy at a rate lower than the utility from day one. No debt, no risk."

---

### Summary and Diagnosis (Minutes 8–11)

After the 5 questions, repeat what you heard before recommending anything.

---

> "Perfect, [NOMBRE], let me summarize what you told me: your bill is around [MONTO], the property is [owned/rented], the roof is [MATERIAL], and the decision is made by [you / you and your spouse / etc.]. Is that right?
>
> [Wait for confirmation]
>
> With that profile, solar makes a lot of sense. For a bill of [MONTO], we would recommend a system of approximately [RANGO kW]. That would cover [PORCENTAJE]% of your consumption and the return on investment would be [AÑOS] years under Law 417."

*(Use the ranges from Module 2 to estimate. If you don't have exact numbers, say "with the photo of your bill I'll confirm the exact estimate within 48 hours.")*

---

### Close — Schedule the Site Visit (Minutes 11–14)

---

> "[NOMBRE], the logical next step is a site visit — we send our technician to measure the roof, assess the orientation, and calculate exact numbers. The visit takes about 30–45 minutes and is completely free of charge.
>
> Would [DÍA] at [HORA] work for you, or do you prefer [DÍA ALTERNATIVO] at [HORA ALTERNATIVA]?"

---

**Rule:** Always offer TWO specific date and time options. Do not say "when is good for you?" — that creates delays.

**If they hesitate:**

> "[NOMBRE], the visit doesn't commit you to anything — it simply allows us to give you real numbers instead of estimates. Which of the two dates works better for you?"

---

### Alternative Close — Direct Proposal (if they already have photos and bill)

If the customer already sent photos of the bill and roof via WhatsApp, you can skip the visit and send a proposal within 48 hours.

---

> "Excellent — with the photo of your bill and the roof photos I already have everything I need to prepare a detailed proposal. I'll send it to you within 48 hours via WhatsApp and email, with the exact savings, investment, and return figures. Does that work for you?"

---

### Friendly Disqualification (when they don't qualify)

If the bill is too low, the roof is unviable, or they are a tenant without the owner involved:

---

> "[NOMBRE], I really appreciate your time. Being honest, with [the bill amount you mentioned / the roof situation / your tenant status], solar wouldn't give you the return you deserve at this point — I'd rather not sell you something that doesn't work for you.
>
> If your consumption goes up in the future, the roof situation changes, or you acquire the property, I'd be happy to revisit. Do you know anyone — a neighbor, a family member, a business — with a higher bill or their own property where solar would make sense?"

---

**Why ask for referrals when disqualifying:** A person you honestly disqualified is more likely to refer you than someone you sold to when it wasn't in their interest.

## Buying Signals — Pay Attention

These phrases or behaviors indicate that the prospect is ready to move forward. Do not overlook them.

1. Mentions they already have a generator (paying for backup power = they hate depending on the grid)
2. Actively complains about the electric bill ("it's sky high," "I don't know how I pay so much")
3. Already researched solar before talking to you
4. Markets with "eco" or "sustainable" to their customers (hotels, lodges, surf camps)
5. Expat owner who compared prices with their home country
6. Currently renovating or expanding the property
7. Asks about the installation process before you bring it up
8. Asks about the system warranty

**When you detect 2 or more signals:** Accelerate the close. Don't ask more unnecessary questions — propose the visit date.

## Qualification and Disqualification Criteria

### Qualifies if ALL of the following apply:
- Monthly bill: $150+ residential / $1,500+ commercial
- Owned property (or tenant with owner involved, lease 5+ years)
- Roof with minimum usable area: 30 m²+ residential / 300 m²+ commercial
- Active electrical connection (distribution grid, eligible for ASEP net metering)
- Decision-maker identified and accessible

### Disqualify (honestly) if:
- Confirmed bill below $80/month residential (return period too long)
- Tenant with fewer than 5 years remaining on lease, without owner involved
- Unviable roof: very small area or permanent all-day shade
- No grid connection and no plan to connect
- Property in legal dispute
- Decision-maker inaccessible after 3 attempts

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Talking more than listening.** The discovery call is 70% questions, 30% talking. If you're doing most of the talking, something is wrong.

2. **Skipping the decision-maker question.** If there is a spouse or business partner who must approve and you don't find out before the proposal, they'll say "let me check with them" and the process drags on for weeks.

3. **Not asking for the bill photo during the call.** The bill is the most important document. Ask for it while the customer is still on the line or in the first minute after hanging up: "We just finished — can you send me the bill photo here?"

4. **Ending the call without a confirmed date.** "I'll let you know" is not a close. If the call ends without a visit date or proposal, the probability of moving forward drops significantly.

5. **Offering only one date.** If you suggest one day and it doesn't work, the prospect says "I'll let you know when I'm free" — and they don't. Always two options.

6. **Not validating before diagnosing.** Before saying "solar is right for you," repeat what you heard: "With a $200 bill, zinc roof, owned property... correct?" If there's an error, better to catch it now.

7. **Pressing on when there are disqualification signals.** If the bill is $40/month, disqualifying honestly is better than continuing the process — it saves time for both of you and builds a reputation for integrity.

8. **Forgetting the visit reminder.** If you scheduled the visit, send a reminder the evening before. Without a reminder, 30% of prospects forget or don't connect the visit with the urgency it has.

## 🎭 Role-Play

**Exercise: The Call That Has Everything**

**Context:** Olga from Las Tablas accepted the 15-minute call. Her bill is between $150–$300 and she wants to move forward as soon as possible. You don't know whether the property is hers or the condition of the roof.

**Characters:**
- **Agent (you):** Follow the 15-minute structure.
- **Olga (partner):** Answer honestly. The roof is zinc, the property has been hers for 10 years, she lives alone, her most recent bill was $180. She is interested in purchasing the system.

**Instructions:**
1. Execute the opening (ask permission to ask questions)
2. Ask the 5 questions in order
3. Deliver the summary/diagnosis out loud
4. Propose the site visit with two date options
5. Ask for the bill photo before hanging up

**The role-play ends when the agent has a confirmed visit date AND has asked for the bill photo.**

**Evaluation checklist:**
- Did they ask the 5 questions in order? (Yes / No)
- Did they validate the summary before diagnosing? (Yes / No)
- Did they close with two date options? (Yes / No)
- Did they ask for the bill photo? (Yes / No)
- Did the call run under 15 minutes? (Yes / No)

## ✅ Checklist — Discovery Call

**During the call:**
- [ ] Opened by asking permission to ask questions
- [ ] Asked the exact bill amount (Question 1)
- [ ] Confirmed whether the property is owned or rented (Question 2)
- [ ] Identified roof material and approximate size (Question 3)
- [ ] Identified all decision-makers (Question 4)
- [ ] Explored budget and/or interest in PPA (Question 5)
- [ ] Delivered the summary out loud before diagnosing

**After the call:**
- [ ] Logged all information in the CRM
- [ ] Sent visit confirmation via WhatsApp with date and time
