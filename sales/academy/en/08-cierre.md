# Module 8: The Close — From "I'm Interested" to "Let's Sign"

> **Level:** Intermediate | **Estimated time:** 90 minutes | **Prerequisite:** Modules 1–7

---

## 🎯 Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Recognize when the prospect is ready to close — the signals that indicate it.
2. Apply 5 closing techniques adapted to the Panamanian culture of Azuero.
3. Create honest urgency without false pressure.
4. Handle "let me think about it" when it happens specifically at the moment of signing.
5. Guide the signing moment with clarity and confidence.
6. Know exactly what you can and what you CANNOT promise as a field salesperson.

## Theory: The Close Is Not a Moment — It's a Process

The most frequent mistake among new salespeople is treating the close as an event that happens at the end of the meeting. In reality, you close at every step of the process: when the prospect accepts the visit, when they hear the full presentation, when they ask specific questions, when they share their bill.

The formal close — the signature — is only the last consequence of many small "yeses" that accumulate throughout the process.

**In Azuero especially:** do not force the close before the prospect is ready. Trust comes first. A rushed close in the local culture can damage the relationship and lose the deal permanently.

---

## Buying Signals: When to Close?

The prospect rarely says "I'm ready to sign." They communicate it through actions and questions. Learn to read those signals.

### Verbal Signals

- **"How long does the installation take?"** — they are visualizing the process on their property.
- **"What exactly is the panel warranty?"** — they are evaluating long-term risk.
- **"How does net metering work in practice?"** — they want to understand the operational details.
- **"Can I pay in installments or some other way?"** — they are thinking about how to finance it.
- **"Do you have clients in Azuero I could contact?"** — they are seeking local social validation.
- **"What if in 5 years I want to expand the system?"** — they are already projecting the future with the system installed.

### Non-Verbal Signals

- Takes photos of the ROI or presentation with their phone.
- Calls their partner, spouse, or accountant to show them what they're seeing.
- Starts calculating out loud: "So in about 3 years I've already recovered it..."
- Leans forward in their chair during the presentation.
- Stops checking their phone and maintains steady eye contact.

### When NOT to Close

- If you just presented the price without having presented the full value.
- If there are unresolved objections — go back to Module 7.
- If the real decision-maker is not present and has not given their approval.
- If the prospect seems rushed, distracted, or uncomfortable.

## The 5 Closing Techniques for the Azuero Market

---

### Technique 1: Summary Close

**When to use it:** After resolving all objections. The prospect has said "yes" to many individual points — it only takes connecting them.

**What it does:** Summarizes the agreements that already exist and turns the "final yes" into a natural continuation of everything before.

**Script:**

> "Don [NOMBRE], today we've looked at several important things: that your $[MONTO_FACTURA] bill can be reduced significantly, that Law 417 lowers the effective installation cost, that we handle all the ASEP paperwork, and that the real payback period is 2.5 to 3.5 years. With that clear picture, what's stopping you from taking the next step today?"

---

### Technique 2: Assumptive Close

**When to use it:** The prospect has given clear signals of interest and there are no outstanding objections.

**What it does:** Speaks as if the decision is already made — directs the conversation toward practical details rather than the decision itself.

**Script:**

> "Very well, Don [NOMBRE]. To prepare the formal proposal I need to confirm two details: your business name or RUC number, and what your current rate is with the utility?"

Or in a softer version:

> "Would it work better for you if we send the proposal to review on Tuesday or Wednesday of this week?"

**Important:** If the prospect says "hold on, I haven't said yes" — don't panic. Respond calmly:

> "You're right, my apologies. What do you need to feel confident moving forward?"

---

### Technique 3: Alternative Close

**When to use it:** The prospect is choosing between two options, or between moving forward and not moving forward.

**What it does:** Offers two concrete options — neither of which is "no."

**Script — payment options:**

> "Would you prefer to start with the direct purchase to maximize your long-term ROI, or does the PPA give you more financial comfort at this moment?"

**Script — follow-up date:**

> "Should we send the proposal to review on Monday, or would you prefer to meet directly on Friday with all the numbers ready?"

**Never ask:** "Do you want to move forward or not?" — that question openly invites a "no."

---

### Technique 4: Law 417 Urgency Close

**When to use it:** The prospect says "it's not the right time" or "later" without a concrete reason.

**What it does:** Creates honest urgency based on real market facts — not artificial pressure.

**Script:**

> "Don [NOMBRE], I understand you want to take your time. I just want to share something I think is important: Law 417, which provides the installation tax exemption, has no confirmed expiration date. When the policy changes — and eventually it will — the effective cost goes up 15 to 20%."

> "Also, every month that passes is $[MONTO_MENSUAL] you're paying the utility instead of your own roof. That monthly cost is real right now. Is that monthly expense worth it to gain more analysis time?"

**Absolute rule:** Only use genuine urgency. Never invent deadlines, temporary discounts, or price threats that don't exist. In Azuero, if they find out, you lose the relationship and your reputation.

---

### Technique 5: Trial Close

**When to use it:** The prospect wants to move forward but feels the commitment is too large all at once.

**What it does:** Reduces the size of the first step to lower the barrier to entry.

**Script:**

> "I understand it's an important decision. How about we take a first step with no commitment: the technical team does the detailed roof evaluation, calculates the exact numbers, and you receive the complete formal proposal. With that in hand, you decide with all the information. If it doesn't convince you, you've lost nothing."

> ⚠️ Before using this close, confirm with management the exact terms of the no-commitment period and what the formal proposal entails. **[VERIFICAR CON GERENTE]**

## "Let Me Think About It" at the Moment of Signing

This objection is different when it appears at the very end — right before signing — than when it comes up during the presentation. At the moment of closing, it almost always means there is ONE final objection the prospect isn't saying out loud.

**The most costly mistake:** Pushing with additional arguments. In Panamanian culture, pressure at the moment of signing can make the prospect cut the relationship entirely.

### The Step-by-Step Protocol

**Step 1 — Don't react with urgency.** Leave a 3–5 second silence. It communicates confidence, not anxiety.

**Step 2 — Validate without conceding:**
> "Of course. This is an important decision and you have every right to take the time you need."

**Step 3 — Explore the final objection:**
> "To help you think it through better — what is it that's generating the most doubt for you right now? Is it the amount, the process, something that wasn't completely clear, or do you need to consult with someone before moving forward?"

**Step 4 — Listen and respond to ONLY that objection.** Do not repeat the full presentation. One point, one response.

**Step 5 — Close again after responding:**
> "With that clarified, what do you still need to move forward today?"

### If They Say "I Need to Consult with Someone"

> "Completely understandable. Who else is involved in this decision? If possible, I'd like to include them in our next conversation so you don't have to explain everything yourself and they can ask questions directly."

**Goal of that moment:** Either resolve the doubt right then, or leave with a specific date for the meeting with the decision-makers — never leave without a next step.

## The Signing Moment: Document Flow

When the prospect says "yes," the process that follows must be clear and smooth. Confusion at the signing moment creates doubt, and doubt can reverse the decision.

### What You Do

1. **Thank them simply and professionally:** "Excellent, Don [NOMBRE]. You're going to see the results." Continue without excessive enthusiasm — the salesperson's emotion can create discomfort for the prospect.

2. **Present the documents in order:** *[VERIFICAR CON GERENTE — exact list of documents required at the time of signing and the correct order of presentation]*

3. **Briefly explain each document before they sign:**
> "This is the [NOMBRE DEL DOCUMENTO] agreement, which establishes [MAIN POINT]. Any questions before you sign it?"

4. **Confirm the initial deposit:** *[VERIFICAR CON GERENTE — amount or percentage of required deposit]*

5. **Explain the next steps clearly:**
> "After this, our technical team will contact you within [PLAZO — VERIFICAR CON GERENTE] to coordinate the detailed technical visit and the final design. From there to installation is approximately [PLAZO — VERIFICAR CON GERENTE]. We handle the entire process with ASEP."

### What You Do NOT Do

- Do not rush the signing — allow the prospect to read what they are signing.
- Do not make additional verbal promises after they sign — what is not in the contract does not exist.
- Do not leave without giving the prospect a written summary of the next steps.
- Do not leave any documents unsigned for "later" — close everything that can be closed in that moment.

## What the Salesperson CANNOT Promise

> This section is critical. Promising something outside your authority damages trust and can legally compromise the company.

| What the prospect may ask | The correct response |
|---|---|
| Exact and final price | "The exact price is confirmed by management with the final technical design — what I gave you today is the field estimate." |
| Exact installation date | "The schedule is confirmed by the technical team — I'll communicate the exact date as soon as the formal proposal is ready." |
| Guarantee of a specific savings amount | "We show you the estimate based on your real consumption. The technician confirms the final number after the detailed evaluation." |
| A price discount | "The price doesn't change — but I can speak with management to see if we can add any service at no additional cost. [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE]" |
| Changes to the standard contract | "Any modification to the standard contract is authorized by management. I'll consult immediately." |
| ASEP approval in X days | "The ASEP process is managed by our team — the timeline depends on ASEP. We'll keep you updated in real time. [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE for typical estimated timeline]" |
| Government subsidy or benefit beyond Law 417 | "The benefits I confirmed are those under Law 417. Any other benefit I'll verify with management before confirming it to you. [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE]" |

**Field golden rule:** When you don't know the answer with certainty, say: *"Let me verify that with management and confirm it with you today."* That phrase is far more powerful than an invented answer that can't be delivered later.

## 📞 Closing Scripts

### Natural Close (clear buying signals present)

> "Don [NOMBRE], with everything we've looked at today — the ROI of approximately [X] years, the Law 417 benefits, and our local team in Azuero — is there anything else you need to feel comfortable moving forward? [Listen and respond if there's anything] Excellent. Shall we get the formal proposal started this week?"

### When They Ask for Additional Time

> "Of course, Don [NOMBRE]. What specific information would help you feel confident about this decision? I'll prepare exactly that for our next conversation. How about we talk on [DÍA] at [HORA]?"

### "Let Me Think About It" at the Moment of Signing

> "Of course. Just to help you think it through — what is it that's generating the most doubt right now: the amount, the process, or do you need to consult with someone?"

### Confirmation After Signing

> "Very good, Don [NOMBRE]. The next steps are: [SUMMARIZE 1-2-3]. Our team will contact you within [PLAZO — VERIFICAR CON GERENTE] to coordinate the technical visit. For any questions along the way, I'm here. Thank you for trusting Solaris."

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes in Closing

1. **Closing too early** — before presenting full value. Price without value guarantees an objection.
2. **Closing too late** — the prospect gives clear buying signals and you keep presenting. When the customer is ready, close.
3. **Reacting with pressure to "let me think about it"** — pressure permanently closes doors in Azuero culture.
4. **Promising what you can't deliver** — to win the signature that day. Broken promises destroy local reputation.
5. **Leaving without a next step if you didn't close** — every visit ends with a specific date, always.
6. **Showing too much excitement when they say yes** — maintain calm and professionalism. Excessive emotion can make the prospect feel they were "sold."
7. **Forgetting to explain the next steps** — the prospect needs to know exactly what happens after signing to feel secure in their decision.

## 🎭 Role-Play: From "Let Me Think About It" to the Close

**Situation:** Don Alfredo, owner of a hotel in Pedasi, has seen the full presentation. All his objections were resolved in Module 7. You ask whether he wants to move forward and he says: "Look, everything you showed me is very good, but let me think about it this week."

**Partner A — Don Alfredo:** Your real objection (which you haven't said) is that you want to discuss it with your business partner in Panama City. If the salesperson asks the right question, you reveal it. If the salesperson pushes without exploring, say "I'd prefer to call later" and end the conversation.

**Partner B — Salesperson:** Apply the complete "let me think about it at close" protocol:
1. 3–5 seconds of silence.
2. Validate without conceding.
3. Explore with an open question.
4. Respond only to what needs to be responded to.
5. Close with a concrete next step — either the signature or a meeting with the partner included.

**Exercise goal:** Leave the meeting with a specific, committed date — not with "I'll think about it and let you know."

**Time:** 15 minutes per round. Switch roles. Discuss: what exploration question worked best to uncover the real objection?
