# Module 9: Follow-Up and CRM

> **Solaris Panama Sales Academy** | Level: All salespeople
> Estimated time: 2 hours of study + script practice

---

## 🎯 Module Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Execute a structured follow-up after every visit or sent proposal.
2. Re-engage a prospect who has gone weeks without responding.
3. Close a lost deal gracefully and keep the door open.
4. Ask for referrals with a clear script, without feeling like you're "bothering."
5. Keep the CRM updated with every contact, status, and next action.

---

## Part 1: Follow-Up — Why Most Salespeople Fail

The sale doesn't die at the visit. It dies in the silence that follows.

In Azuero, prospects are busy people: they have farms, businesses, families. Not responding doesn't mean they're not interested. It means their life moved on and you fell into the background. The salesperson's job is to stay present without becoming a nuisance.

**Solaris golden rule:** Every visit or proposal generates a follow-up commitment. If there is no next contact scheduled in the CRM, the deal is dead.

### Standard Post-Proposal Sequence

| Time | Action | Channel | Goal |
|------|--------|---------|------|
| T+0 (same day as visit) | Visit close message | WhatsApp | Leave a good impression, confirm the proposal arrived |
| T+48h (if no response) | Value follow-up | WhatsApp | Resolve a specific doubt, no pressure |
| T+5 days | Soft urgency follow-up | WhatsApp | Remind them that the installation schedule fills up |
| T+10 days | "Last call" message | WhatsApp + email | Definitive close or transition to lost deal |
| T+30 days | Monthly reactivation | WhatsApp | For deals on "pause," without abandoning them |

## Part 2: Post-Visit Follow-Up Scripts

### 2.1 — Immediate Message (same day as the visit)

Send this within 2 hours of finishing the visit. While the impression is still fresh.

---
**T+0 Script:**

> Dear [Nombre], it was a pleasure visiting you today in [lugar]. I now have all the data I need to prepare your proposal.
>
> I'll send you the complete analysis with real numbers for your property before [fecha — máximo 48h]. If you have any questions in the meantime, I'm right here.
>
> Have a wonderful afternoon.
>
> — Henry, Solaris Panama

---

**Why it works:** Confirms the visit, sets a concrete date for the proposal, and asks for nothing. The prospect is left with a positive expectation.

---

### 2.2 — Follow-Up at 48h (no response to the proposal)

---
**T+48h Script:**

> [Nombre], I'm confirming the proposal reached you via [WhatsApp/email]. I wanted to check if you had a chance to review it or if there's any point you'd like to clarify.
>
> The most common question I get at this stage is about the ASEP process and how long it takes. If that's generating any doubt, I'm happy to explain it in 5 minutes by phone.

---

**Note:** Don't say "What did you decide?" yet. Offer value (resolving a doubt). The decision comes after.

---

### 2.3 — Follow-Up at 5 Days (soft urgency)

---
**T+5 Days Script:**

> [Nombre], I'm writing because we're coordinating the installation schedule for next quarter. Projects in [zona — ej. "Las Tablas"] are being booked now.
>
> I don't want to pressure you — if you're still evaluating, that's fine. I just wanted you to know that slots fill up and I'd prefer to reserve space for you if you're planning to move forward.
>
> Are you still planning to proceed?

---

**Why it works:** Introduces real (not invented) urgency and asks for a simple confirmation, not a signature. Many prospects respond here.

---

### 2.4 — "Last Call" Message (T+10 Days)

---
**T+10 Days Script:**

> [Nombre], this will be my last message for now — I don't want to be a bother.
>
> If the timing isn't right, I completely understand. I remain at your disposal whenever you need. If at any point you change your mind or want to pick this back up, just write to me.
>
> It was a pleasure meeting you. I wish you all the best.
>
> — Henry

---

**Important:** This message works precisely because it doesn't pressure. Many prospects respond here saying "give me one more month." That is a win — move them to monthly reactivation in the CRM.

## Part 3: Re-Engaging Stalled Deals

A stalled deal is one that has had no movement for more than 3 weeks but is not formally lost. In Azuero this happens often: the prospect said "yes, yes" at the visit, but life got in the way.

### When to Re-Engage

- The prospect said "let me think about it" and more than 2 weeks have passed without a response.
- The proposal was "seen" (WhatsApp shows double blue check) but not commented on.
- The prospect asked to "call them in a month" and that time has passed.

### Monthly Reactivation Script

---
**Reactivation script:**

> [Nombre], I'm writing because I thought of you. I just finished an installation in [zona cercana — ej. "Chitré"] and the client brought their bill down from $280 to $40 in the first month.
>
> If you'd like, I can share the case with you — sometimes seeing real numbers from someone in the area helps make the decision.
>
> No commitment, just in case it's useful.

---

**Variant for prospects with financial objections:**

> [Nombre], good afternoon. I wanted to mention that starting this month we're offering the PPA model in your area — zero upfront payment, and from the first month you're already paying less for electricity than with the utility. Would you like me to explain it?

---

**Rule:** A stalled deal receives one reactivation message per month, no more. If after 3 reactivations there is still no response, move to "lost deal" and free your mental energy.

## Part 4: Handling a Lost Deal

Losing a deal is part of the job. What matters is how you leave.

In a tight-knit community like Azuero, how you say goodbye to a prospect matters as much as how you greeted them. A prospect who "didn't buy now" might be a client in 18 months, or might refer their neighbor tomorrow.

### Lost Deal Script

---
**When the prospect says "I decided not to move forward":**

> [Nombre], that's completely understandable — the timing isn't always right, and I respect that.
>
> I just ask one favor: if at any point your situation changes, or if you know someone who is thinking about solar for their home or business, please think of us.
>
> It was a pleasure meeting you and I wish you every success. Whenever you'd like to revisit, we'll be here.
>
> — Henry, Solaris Panama

---

**What NOT to say when losing a deal:**
- "Can I ask why not?" (pressures, makes them uncomfortable)
- "But the numbers clearly work for you..." (judges their decision)
- "Give me another chance to explain it?" (sounds desperate)

Saying goodbye with class is free advertising. In Las Tablas, everyone knows everyone.

## Part 5: Asking for Referrals

In Azuero, referrals are more valuable than any Google ad. A person who tells their neighbor "the Solaris rep treated me really well" is worth more than $500 in advertising.

**When to ask for referrals:**
1. Right after closing a deal (the client is excited).
2. When the installation is finished and the client sees the system working.
3. When the client receives their first reduced bill.
4. When a prospect says "no" in a friendly way (see previous section).

### Referral Script — Post-Close

---
**Script (after signing the contract):**

> [Nombre], great news that we're going to work together. While we coordinate the technical details, let me ask you a question:
>
> Do you know anyone else — a neighbor, a family member, a business partner — who is also thinking about lowering their electricity bill? You don't need to convince them of anything, I just introduce the idea and handle the rest.
>
> At Solaris we have a program: if a referral of yours closes a contract, you receive a credit of [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE] toward your next maintenance service.

---

### Referral Script — Post-Installation (first bill)

---
> [Nombre], so great that you saw your first reduced bill. How much did it come in compared to before?
>
> [Listen to the number — celebrate with the client]
>
> Hey, do you know anyone who could also benefit? In this area very few people still know what they could be saving. If you give me a name and number, I'll contact them with your permission and explain it.

---

**The name + number technique:** Don't ask them to reach out themselves. Ask for the name and number and say you'll make the first contact. That way the client doesn't have to do any work. The conversion rate for referrals that turn into contact goes up significantly when you handle the first step.

## Part 6: CRM — Basic Hygiene

The CRM is your memory. If it's not in the CRM, it doesn't exist.

### The 5 CRM Commandments at Solaris

1. **Every contact gets logged.** A call, a WhatsApp, a visit — everything goes into the CRM with the date, channel, and what was discussed in 1–2 lines.

2. **The lead status is always current.** Use only the system statuses:
   - `new` — the form came in, not yet contacted
   - `contacted` — already received the first message
   - `qualified` — passed the discovery: bill and roof confirmed
   - `cold` / `warm` / `hot` — prospect's level of interest
   - `proposal_sent` — proposal is in the prospect's hands
   - `won` — contract signed (always log the `deal_value`)
   - `lost` — disqualified (always with the reason in the notes)
   - `Vendor` / `Partner` / `Not a Lead` — contacts that are not sales prospects (hidden by default)

   > Visit scheduling and negotiation dates are handled as **next actions with a date**, not as status. See **Module 11 — The Solaris CRM** for the step-by-step walkthrough on screen.

3. **There is always a "next step" with a date.** Never leave a conversation without knowing when you will reach out again. If the prospect says "call me in two weeks" — schedule it in the CRM for 14 days from now.

4. **Notes are concrete.** Don't write "called and talked." Write: "15-min call. Confirmed zinc roof ~80 m². Husband makes the final decision. Says they need to discuss it. Follow-up Thursday."

5. **If the CRM has no next action, the deal freezes.** Review at the start of each day the deals that have a follow-up due that day.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why it's a problem | What to do instead |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Not sending the T+0 the same day as the visit | The prospect forgets who you are after 48h | Send messages from the car before you leave |
| Asking "what did you decide?" at T+48h | Pressures, makes them uncomfortable, feels like being chased | Offer to resolve a specific doubt |
| Leaving deals without a status or next action | Miss deals that could have closed weeks later | Before closing the CRM, verify every deal has a status + date |
| Not asking for referrals because it "feels awkward" | The main source of business in Azuero is referrals | Practice the script until it feels natural |
| Closing the lost deal with a reproach | Damages your reputation in a small community | Always say goodbye with warmth and an open door |
| Following up every day with the same prospect | Looks desperate, the prospect blocks you | Respect the timing: T+0, T+48h, T+5d, T+10d |

## 🎭 Module Role-Play

### Scenario A: Stalled Deal

**Context:** You visited Carlos 3 weeks ago. The proposal was sent. Double blue check on WhatsApp but zero response.

- **Salesperson:** Execute the monthly reactivation script with a real case from the area.
- **Evaluator:** Grade whether they mentioned the success case, whether they were direct but not pushy, and whether they ended with a simple yes/no question.

### Scenario B: Lost Deal + Referral Request

**Context:** Doña María just told you "I decided not to install for now, my husband isn't convinced."

- **Salesperson:** Close the deal with grace and ask for a referral before hanging up.
- **Evaluator:** Grade whether there was no reproach, whether the goodbye was warm, and whether the referral script sounded natural or forced.

### Scenario C: CRM Live

**Context:** The manager takes the salesperson's CRM at random and reviews 5 deals.

- **Salesperson:** Explains the status of each one, when the last contact was, and what the next step is.
- **Evaluator:** Grades whether each deal has an updated status, a descriptive note, and a next action with a date.

## ✅ Module 9 Checklist

Before ending your day, verify:

- [ ] Every visit today has a T+0 message sent.
- [ ] Every deal in the CRM has an updated status.
- [ ] No deal is missing a "next action" with a set date.
- [ ] Deals scheduled for follow-up today were contacted.
- [ ] No deal exceeds 10 days without contact without being marked as "Nurture" or "Lost."
- [ ] If you closed any deal (won or lost), you asked for referrals.
