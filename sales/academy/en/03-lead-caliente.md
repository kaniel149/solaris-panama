# Module 3: Hot Lead — The First to Call Wins

> **Level:** Beginner | **Estimated time:** 60 minutes | **Prerequisite:** Modules 1 and 2

---

## 🎯 Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Identify what a "hot lead" is and why response time is the most important factor
2. Send the first WhatsApp message within the first 5 minutes of receiving the lead
3. Execute the 60-second call script word for word
4. Follow the 4-touch sequence when there is no response
5. Lock in a confirmed date and time for the discovery call

---

## Theory: What Is a Hot Lead?

A **hot lead** is someone who filled out the form on solaris-panama.com — they came through a Google or Meta ad and left their name, phone number and electricity bill range. They already showed interest. Our job is to contact them before that interest cools off.

**The golden rule: contact within 5 minutes.**

Every hour that passes without contact, the probability of a response drops. A lead who filled out the form at 2 PM must receive a message before 2:05 PM.

### Why WhatsApp First?

In Panama, WhatsApp is the number one communication channel. People don't answer calls from unknown numbers, but they do read WhatsApp messages. The sequence is always:

1. WhatsApp (first touch, always)
2. If no response: phone call (only after T+24h)
3. Email as backup (if they have a registered email)

### The Complete Hot Lead Cycle

```
Form filled out
      ↓
WhatsApp T+0 (within 5 min)
      ↓
Did they respond?
  ├── YES → 60-sec call → Schedule 15-min call
  └── NO → T+24h → T+72h → T+7d → Monthly nurture list
```

### The 4 Touches — Universal Sequence

| Touch | When | Channel | Goal |
|-------|------|---------|------|
| **T+0** | Within 5 minutes | WhatsApp (primary) / Email if unavailable | Introduce yourself, acknowledge the form, propose a 15-min call |
| **T+24h** | If no response | WhatsApp | Share a concrete number tied to their bill amount |
| **T+72h** | If still no response | WhatsApp + Email | Local social proof + soft urgency with Law 417 |
| **T+7d** | If no response to any touch | WhatsApp | Polite close, open door, move to nurture |

**Best hours in Panama:** Tuesday through Thursday, 8:00-10:00 AM or 2:00-4:00 PM.
Avoid: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, and national holidays.

## 📞 Scripts

### Script 1 — WhatsApp, Touch 1 (T+0) — Residential Lead

Customize the brackets before sending. Never send a generic message.

---

> Good morning [NOMBRE], this is [AGENTE] from Solaris Panama. We received your inquiry about solar panels for your [home / property] — thank you for reaching out.
>
> I saw that your bill is in the range of [RANGO FACTURA] per month. For that level, we typically recommend a 5-7 kW system that covers 70-90% of consumption, tax-exempt under Law 417.
>
> Would it work for you if we set up a 15-minute call this week to go over the real numbers for your property?

---

### Script 2 — 60-Second Phone Call

Use this script when the lead responds to the WhatsApp and agrees to talk, or when they call in directly.

---

> "Good morning, am I speaking with [NOMBRE]? Great.
>
> This is [AGENTE] from Solaris Panama. We received your solar panel request and wanted to confirm it came through.
>
> Before anything else, one quick question: roughly how much was your last electricity bill?"
>
> [Wait for the client's response]
>
> "Perfect. With that amount I can tell you that solar makes a lot of sense — at that range we recover the investment in 4-5 years, and with Law 417 the system comes in tax-exempt.
>
> Do you have 15 minutes tomorrow morning, or does this afternoon work better for you?"

---

**Target duration: 60 seconds to get the "yes" for the call.**

The goal of this first contact is NOT to explain solar. It is only to book the 15-minute call. Don't sell the system yet.

---

### Script 3 — WhatsApp, Touch 2 (T+24h, no response)

---

> [NOMBRE], here's a concrete figure: for a bill of [MONTO] per month, we typically recommend a [5-7 kW / 8-10 kW] system. Approximate investment [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE], savings of [VERIFICAR CON GERENTE] from the first month, and it pays for itself in 4-5 years under Law 417.
>
> If you share a photo of your last bill here, I'll put together a personalized analysis within 48 hours. Does that work?

---

### Script 4 — WhatsApp, Touch 3 (T+72h, no response)

---

> [NOMBRE], we're closing our visit schedule in the Azuero area for this week and next. If you'd like us to stop by and assess your roof with no commitment, just let me know and we'll coordinate it.
>
> If you'd prefer to wait, that's completely fine — I'm here whenever the timing is right for you.

---

### Script 5 — WhatsApp, Touch 4 (T+7d — "Breakup")

---

> [NOMBRE], it seems like now might not be the right time — totally understandable. I'm here whenever you need it. Have a great week.

---

After Touch 4: add the lead to the monthly nurture list. **Do not delete the contact.** Leads who say "not now" sometimes buy in 3-6 months.

---

### How to Confirm the Discovery Call

When the lead says "yes" to talking, lock in the date in the same message. Don't leave the door open.

---

> "Perfect, [NOMBRE]. Does tomorrow [DÍA] at [HORA] work for you, or would you prefer [DÍA ALTERNATIVO] at [HORA ALTERNATIVA]? I'll send you a reminder here the night before."

---

**The two-option rule:** Always offer TWO specific dates and times. Never ask "when is a good time for you?" — that creates delays because the lead has to think and then responds "I'll let you know."

**Reminder the night before:**

> "Hi [NOMBRE], just confirming: tomorrow [DÍA] at [HORA] we have our 15-minute call — does that still work for you?"

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Calling first, without a prior WhatsApp.** In Panama nobody answers calls from unknown numbers. The first touch is always WhatsApp.

2. **Waiting more than 5 minutes for the first contact.** Every hour that passes reduces the chances of a response. If the lead comes in at 3 PM, contact them before 3:05 PM.

3. **Trying to sell solar in the first message.** The goal of T+0 is only to get the "yes" for a 15-minute call — not to explain panels, prices or Law 417 in detail.

4. **Skipping touches to "avoid bothering them."** The 4-touch sequence is the minimum of the process. Skipping T+72h or T+7d reduces conversions. The lead filled out a form — they expect contact.

5. **Not offering two time slot options.** Asking "when is a good time for you?" without giving options results in "I'll let you know" and the lead never confirms.

6. **Sending generic information before knowing the bill amount.** The bill is the number one data point. Without that number, you can't personalize the message or give concrete figures.

7. **Forgetting the reminder the night before.** If you confirmed a call and don't send a reminder, 30% of leads forget. Always send the reminder.

8. **Leaving a voicemail on the first call attempt.** If you call and they don't answer, don't leave a voicemail. The WhatsApp you already sent says more than a voicemail does.

## 🎭 Role-Play

**Exercise: The Interested but Busy Lead**

**Context:** Ivette filled out the form yesterday. Her bill is between $150-$300 per month. Her timeline is 1-3 months. You just received the lead notification.

**Characters:**
- **Agent (you):** Just saw the lead notification. It is 9:15 AM.
- **Ivette (colleague):** Responds to the WhatsApp but says "yes I'm interested, but I'm very busy with work this week."

**Instructions for the agent:**
1. Send Touch 1 via WhatsApp (you may read it from the script)
2. When Ivette responds, transition to the 60-second call
3. Handle the objection "I'm really busy this week"
4. Close with two time slot options for the following week

**The role-play ends when the agent has a confirmed date and time.**

**Evaluation checklist:**
- Did they personalize the message with the name and bill range? (Yes / No)
- Did they mention Law 417? (Yes / No)
- Did they offer two date and time options? (Yes / No)
- Did the call last less than 2 minutes? (Yes / No)
- Did they ask about the bill? (Yes / No)

## ✅ Checklist — For Every Hot Lead

**Touch 1 (T+0):**
- [ ] Received the lead notification and reacted within 5 minutes
- [ ] Personalized the message with the lead's name and their bill range
- [ ] Sent the WhatsApp (did not call first)
- [ ] If they responded: proposed two time slot options for the 15-min call
- [ ] If they didn't respond: scheduled T+24h for the following day during the 8-10 AM or 2-4 PM window

**Follow-up:**
- [ ] Logged the lead in the CRM with the current date and status
- [ ] If a call was confirmed: scheduled a reminder for the night before
- [ ] If T+7d was reached without response: moved to the monthly nurture list (without deleting)
