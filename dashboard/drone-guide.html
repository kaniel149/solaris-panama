PRE-FLIGHT

Pre-Flight Checklist

Complete every item before launching. Panama's tropical weather can change rapidly — always check conditions within 30 minutes of flight.

🌤️ Weather Conditions

  • No rain or drizzle — moisture damages gimbal and electronics
  • Wind speed below 25 km/h (check at altitude, not ground level)
  • No thunderstorms within 30km radius — check radar
  • Visibility > 3km — avoid haze and heavy cloud cover
  • Best time: 8:00–10:30 AM or 2:30–4:30 PM (minimal shadows)

🔋 Equipment Check

  • All batteries charged to 100% (minimum 2 batteries per site)
  • SD card formatted and empty — minimum 64GB capacity
  • GPS lock acquired (minimum 8 satellites for accuracy)
  • Propellers inspected — no cracks, chips, or warping
  • Firmware updated on drone and controller
  • Camera lens clean — no dust, smudges, or water spots

📍 Area Assessment

  • Identify safe takeoff/landing zone — flat, clear area minimum 3m × 3m
  • Map overhead obstacles: power lines, tall trees, antennas, light poles
  • Check for nearby airports — maintain 5km clearance (see Panama regulations)
  • Notify property owner and neighbors about drone operation
  • Set Return-to-Home altitude above tallest obstacle + 10m margin
FLIGHT PROTOCOL

Flight Protocol

Two-pass approach: nadir (straight-down) for measurements, then oblique for obstacle and shading identification. Both passes are critical for complete survey data.

Nadir Pass — 30m AGL

Fly at 30 meters above ground level with camera pointed straight down (90°). Use automated grid/waypoint mode. Maintain 70% front overlap and 65% side overlap between images. This produces the orthomosaic map used for precise roof measurements and panel layout.

Oblique Pass — 20m AGL

Lower to 20 meters and capture images at 45° angle orbiting the structure. This reveals roof edge conditions, gutter profiles, mounting surfaces, and obstacles that are invisible from directly above. Capture all four compass directions.

Detail Shots

Manually capture close-up photos of: electrical panel location, meter, existing conduit paths, roof penetrations (vents, pipes, skylights), structural connections, and any damage or deterioration. These are essential for engineering design.

Surrounding Area

Fly to 50m AGL and capture wide-angle shots showing the property in context: neighboring structures that may cast shadows, tree canopy extent, access roads for installation vehicles, and compass orientation reference points.

REGULATIONS

Panama Drone Regulations

All commercial drone operations in Panama are regulated by the Dirección General de Aeronáutica Civil (DGAC). Compliance is mandatory for Solaris operations.

⚠️ Legal Requirements

  • All commercial drone operations require DGAC registration
  • Pilot must hold a valid DGAC remote pilot certificate
  • Liability insurance is required for commercial operations
  • Flight plans must be filed for operations in controlled airspace

Operational Limits

  • Maximum altitude: 120m (400ft) above ground level
  • Visual line of sight (VLOS) must be maintained at all times
  • No-fly zones: within 5km of airports and military installations
  • Daylight operations only (sunrise to sunset)
  • No flights over crowds or gatherings
  • Minimum 30m horizontal distance from people not involved

Key Airports Near Azuero

  • Chitré (CTD) — Captain Manuel Niño Airport
  • Pedasí (PDM) — Pedasí Airstrip
  • Las Tablas — local airstrip
  • Always verify restricted zones on DGAC maps before each flight
PHOTO REQUIREMENTS

Photo & Image Requirements

Every survey image must be usable for engineering design. Follow these standards to ensure consistent, high-quality deliverables.

📸 Camera Settings

  • Shoot in RAW + JPEG for maximum post-processing flexibility
  • ISO: Auto (100–400 preferred to minimize noise)
  • Shutter speed: 1/500s minimum to eliminate motion blur
  • White balance: Daylight or Auto (consistent across all images)
  • GPS metadata must be embedded in every image (EXIF)

Required Captures

  • Compass orientation markers visible in overview shots
  • All roof edges clearly visible and identifiable
  • Obstacle identification: trees, poles, structures within 15m
  • Roof material and condition documentation
  • Existing electrical infrastructure (panels, meters, conduits)
  • Shadow-casting elements at different times of day

Quality Standards

  • No motion blur — reject and retake any blurred images
  • Consistent lighting — avoid mixed sun/shadow conditions
  • Complete coverage — no gaps in grid pattern
  • Ground reference: include a known measurement (tape measure or marker) for scale calibration
  • Minimum 100 images per residential site, 300+ for commercial

📁 File Naming Convention

Format: SOLARIS-[CLIENT]-[DATE]-[TYPE]-[SEQ]. Example: SOLARIS-MARTINEZ-20260309-NADIR-001.jpg. This ensures traceability across all projects and simplifies integration with design software.
