PRE-FLIGHT

Pre-Flight Checklist

Complete every item before launch. In Panama's tropical conditions, preparation prevents costly re-surveys.

🌤️ Weather Conditions

  • No rain — clear or partly cloudy ideal
  • Wind speed < 25 km/h (check windguru.com)
  • Best hours: 7-10 AM or 3-5 PM (soft shadows)
  • Avoid midday — harsh shadows hide obstacles
  • Check for rainy season patterns (May-Dec)

🔋 Equipment Check

  • All batteries fully charged (min. 2 batteries)
  • SD card formatted and inserted (min. 64GB)
  • Propellers inspected for damage
  • Controller/phone charged and paired
  • GPS lock confirmed (min. 10 satellites)
  • Firmware updated to latest version

📋 Site Preparation

  • Homeowner/client informed and permission obtained
  • Identify launch/landing zone (flat, clear area)
  • Note power lines, trees, antennas on site
  • Check for nearby no-fly zones (airports, military)
  • Bring reference object for scale (1m ruler)

📐 Data Preparation

  • Record client address and GPS coordinates
  • Note existing roof material (concrete, metal, tile)
  • Approximate roof area from Google Maps
  • Know electric bill kWh (ask client beforehand)
  • Camera set to highest resolution (RAW + JPEG)
FLIGHT PROTOCOL

Flight Protocol

Follow these four passes to capture all necessary data for accurate solar design.

1

Overhead Nadir Shots

Fly directly above the roof at 30m altitude. Camera pointing straight down (90°). Capture systematic grid pattern covering the entire roof area.

Altitude: 30m Angle: 90° (straight down) Overlap: 70% ~15-20 photos
2

Oblique Angle Shots

Lower to 20m and tilt camera to 45°. Circle the roof from all four cardinal directions.

Altitude: 20m Angle: 45° 4 directions (N/E/S/W) ~12-16 photos
3

Obstacle Panorama

From 25m altitude, capture a 360° panorama showing all shading sources around the site.

Altitude: 25m 360° coverage Panorama mode Note all obstructions
4

Detail & Context Shots

Capture close-ups of electrical panel, meter box, roof condition, and a wide context shot of the property.

Various altitudes Close-up + wide ~8-12 photos

MEASUREMENTS

Measurement from Images

Extract accurate roof dimensions, orientation, and tilt from drone imagery.

📏 Roof Dimensions

Use nadir photos with known reference. Software auto-generates measurements.

Area = L × W (from calibrated pixel count)

🧭 Azimuth (Orientation)

Extract from EXIF compass data. Panama optimal: 180° (true south).

Optimal Azimuth (Panama): 180° ± 30°

📐 Tilt Estimation

Compare oblique shots from opposite sides. Typical Panama roofs: 10-25°.

Tilt = arctan(Δh / d) → Optimal: 8-15° for Panama

Panama Drone Regulations

DGAC — Dirección General de Aeronáutica Civil de Panamá governs all drone operations.

Registration

Commercial use requires DGAC registration. Submit specs, ID, insurance. 15-30 business days.

Max Altitude

120m (400ft) AGL maximum. Solar surveys at 20-30m are well within limits.

No-Fly Zones

5km from airports (Tocumen, Albrook, David). No crowds, government, military areas.

Visual Line of Sight

Maintain VLOS at all times. No autonomous beyond-visual flights without authorization.

Insurance

Liability insurance required. Min $50K coverage. Local: ASSA, Mapfre Panama.

Daylight Only

Daylight only. Panama: sunrise ~6 AM, sunset ~6:30 PM year-round.

Survey Report Template

Every drone survey should produce a professional report with these deliverables:

01

Annotated Photos

  • Labeled roof sections with dimensions
  • Marked obstacles and shading objects
  • Electrical entry points highlighted
  • Compass bearing on each image
02

Measurements

  • Total usable roof area (m²)
  • Each roof plane: L × W × pitch
  • Azimuth per roof section
  • Setback distances from edges
03

Shading Analysis

  • Shadow map at 9AM, 12PM, 3PM
  • Identified shading sources + height
  • Annual shading loss estimate (%)
  • Recommended panel placement zones
04

Recommendation

  • Recommended system size (kWp)
  • Estimated panel count and layout
  • Structural concerns (if any)
  • Go / No-Go assessment with rationale
