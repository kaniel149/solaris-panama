PANAMA SPECIFICS

Regulatory & Local Details

Key Panama Requirements

ENSA Net Metering

Up to 500 kW self-consumption. Bidirectional meter required. Excess energy credited on next bill (not paid out). Apply with system design + structural certification. ENSA covers 46.6% of distributed solar customers nationwide.

ASEP License

ASEP (Autoridad Nacional de los Servicios Públicos) regulates all electricity generation. Required for grid-connected systems. Sets technical standards for connection. Installer must hold valid ASEP credentials.

Ley 417 Paperwork

File with customs for import duty exemption. Separate filing for ITBMS (7%) waiver. Selective consumption tax (5%) automatically eliminated. Income tax deduction claimed in annual filing. Processing time varies: $600–$1,200 cost.

Equipment Supply Chain

Import via Colón Free Zone or direct from manufacturers. LONGi has 50 MW supply deal with CELTEC in Panama. Standard lead time: 2–4 weeks. Tier-1 panels (LONGi, Jinko, Canadian Solar, Trina) + Huawei, SolarEdge, or Solis inverters.

Azuero Solar Conditions

5.3 kWh/m²/day average irradiance — excellent for solar. 12 months of usable sun (tropical, near-equatorial). Less seasonal variation than temperate zones. Consistent year-round production. Ideal conditions for reliable ROI calculations.

Distributor Regions

ENSA (Naturgy) — Panama City, Azuero, most of country. EDEMET — Western Panama City metro. EDECHI — Chiriquí, Bocas del Toro. For Azuero: always ENSA. All net metering applications go through ENSA.
