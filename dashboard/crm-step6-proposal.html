Proposal Template Structure

Every proposal follows this 7-section format for consistency and professionalism.

1

Cover Page

First impression matters. Branded, professional, personalized.

  • Solaris Energy logo & brand colors
  • Client name & property address
  • Proposal date & validity (30 days)
  • Unique proposal reference number
2

Executive Summary

One page that tells the whole story. Client's problem → our solution → their savings.

  • Current monthly bill & annual electricity cost
  • Proposed system size & expected generation
  • Estimated monthly & annual savings
  • Payback period & 25-year ROI
3

Technical Specifications

Equipment details that build trust and justify pricing.

  • Panel brand, model, wattage, efficiency
  • Inverter brand, model, type (string/micro)
  • Mounting system & materials
  • Monitoring system included
4

Financial Analysis

The numbers that close the deal. Show clear ROI and payback.

  • System cost breakdown (equipment, labor, permits)
  • 25-year savings projection table
  • Payback calculation with rate escalation
  • Comparison: with solar vs. without solar
5

Project Timeline

Clear milestones so clients know what to expect and when.

  • Contract signing → Design finalization (1 week)
  • ENSA application & permits (2-4 weeks)
  • Equipment procurement (1-2 weeks)
  • Installation (1-3 days residential, 1-2 weeks commercial)
  • ENSA inspection & meter swap (1-2 weeks)
6

Warranty & Guarantees

Triple-layer warranty gives clients confidence to invest.

  • 25-year panel performance warranty (80% at year 25)
  • 10-year inverter manufacturer warranty
  • 5-year installation workmanship warranty
  • Monitoring included for first year
7

Terms & Conditions

Clear terms protect both parties and prevent disputes.

  • Proposal validity: 30 calendar days
  • Payment schedule: 50/40/10
  • Scope of work boundaries
  • Cancellation policy & change order process

Ley 417 — The Solar Law

Panama's renewable energy law. Include this section in every proposal to educate and build confidence.

Ley 417 — Distributed Generation

Law 417 of 2013 regulates self-generation and net metering in Panama

  • ⚡ Net metering: excess energy credits roll over monthly for up to 3 years
  • 🏠 Systems up to 500 kWp for residential and commercial self-consumption
  • 📋 Simplified interconnection process through local distribution company (ENSA)
  • 💰 No additional taxes on self-generated energy — zero ITBMS on solar generation
  • 🔒 Right to install solar panels on your own property is protected by law
  • 📊 ASEP (regulator) oversees fair implementation and dispute resolution

Include in Every Proposal

"Your solar installation is fully supported by Panama's Ley 417, which guarantees your right to generate your own electricity, receive credit for excess energy through net metering, and connect seamlessly to the ENSA grid. This law provides the legal framework that protects your investment for decades to come."

Follow-up Sequence

Systematic follow-up turns proposals into contracts. Never let a proposal go cold.

📤
Day 0 — Send Proposal

Proposal Delivery

Send via email + WhatsApp. Request read confirmation. Offer to walk through together via call.

"Hi [Name], I've prepared your personalized solar proposal. Would you like me to walk you through the numbers? I can call in the next hour or schedule a convenient time."
📞
Day 2 — First Follow-up

Value-Add Check-in

Don't ask "did you review?" Instead, add value. Share a relevant case study or answer anticipated questions.

"Hi [Name], I wanted to share something interesting — one of our clients in [similar neighborhood] just saved $85 on their first month. Their system is similar to what we proposed for you. Any questions I can help with?"
💬
Day 5 — Urgency Follow-up

Create Gentle Urgency

Mention equipment availability, upcoming price changes, or the next electricity bill cycle.

"Quick update: we have the panels for your system in stock now, but our next shipment isn't until [date]. If you'd like to move forward, we can lock in the current pricing and start the ENSA process this week."
⏰
Day 7 — Final Follow-up

Decision Point

Respectful final touch. Ask for a clear yes/no. If no, ask what would change their mind — valuable feedback.

"Hi [Name], I know life gets busy. I just want to make sure — is solar still something you're considering? If the timing isn't right now, I completely understand. Just let me know either way so I can plan accordingly."
