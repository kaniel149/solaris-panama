FINANCING OPTIONS

How Clients Pay for Solar in Panama

Panama's solar market is still predominantly cash-based, but the Ley 417 tax incentives create a powerful financial case. Here are all current and emerging options.

☰
MOST COMMON

Cash Purchase

The dominant financing method in Panama. Residential systems: $3,000–$15,000. Commercial: $20,000–$80,000. Simple, fast, no interest costs. Combined with Ley 417 tax exemptions, effective cost drops 15–25%.

⚖
KEY INCENTIVE

Ley 417 Tax Benefits

Enacted December 2023. Eliminates import duties, 7% ITBMS (VAT), and 5% selective consumption tax on all solar equipment. Full investment deductible from income tax. Applies to residential, commercial, and generation.

★
AVAILABLE

Bank Loans

Commercial loans via Banco General and Banistmo. Typical rates: 5–7% interest. Primarily for commercial clients. No dedicated solar loan products yet — structured as general business/equipment loans.

●
RURAL AREAS

IDB Rural Electrification

$20M Inter-American Development Bank program for remote areas. Low-interest loans for off-grid and rural solar. Relevant for farms, indigenous communities, and remote tourism operations in Azuero.

⏲
COMING SOON

Solar Leasing

Not yet available in Panama. Opportunity for Solaris to be first mover — partner with leasing companies to offer $0-down solar. Client pays monthly, owns system after term.

⚡
FUTURE MODEL

Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)

Solaris owns the system, client buys electricity at a discount. Zero upfront cost. Ideal for commercial properties and hotels. Requires scale to be profitable — target for Phase 2.

Requirements for Ley 417 Benefits

ASEP license (grid-connected generation) • Secretaría Nacional de Energía certification (self-consumption) • Professional electrical and structural plans • Bidirectional meter + ENSA/Naturgy approval for net metering • Self-consumption limit: up to 500 kW • Processing cost: $600–$1,200 depending on province and scale.

CLIENT GUIDANCE

When to Pay Cash vs. Finance

● Pay Cash When...

  • System under $15,000 (residential)
  • Client has available capital
  • Want fastest possible ROI (2.5–3.5 years)
  • Business qualifies for full Ley 417 income tax deduction
  • Expat/retiree with savings wanting to reduce bills
  • Want zero ongoing financial commitment

★ Consider Financing When...

  • System over $30,000 (large commercial)
  • Capital better deployed elsewhere in business
  • Hotel/resort wanting $0-down (future PPA)
  • Remote/rural property (IDB program eligible)
  • Multiple properties in portfolio
  • Loan rate (5–7%) << electricity cost increase (2–3%/yr)
