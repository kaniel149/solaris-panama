Ley 417 — Panama's Solar Incentive

Law 417 of December 2023 establishes a framework for distributed generation of renewable energy in Panama. It allows net metering for systems up to 500kW and provides significant tax benefits for qualifying installations.

STEP 1

ASEP Registration

Register as a solar energy installer with the Autoridad Nacional de los Servicios Públicos (ASEP). This is the regulatory body overseeing all energy services in Panama.

1

Company Formation

  • Panamanian S.A. or SRL entity
  • Registered with Registro Público
  • Tax ID (RUC) from DGI
  • Aviso de Operación (business license)
2

Technical Documentation

  • Electrical engineer credentials (licensed in Panama)
  • Portfolio of prior installations (if any)
  • Equipment certifications (IEC, UL)
  • Insurance: liability + workers' compensation
3

ASEP Application

  • Submit Solicitud de Registro to ASEP
  • Include technical capacity proof
  • Pay registration fee (~$500)
  • Processing time: 2-4 weeks
STEP 2

Distributor Approval

Each solar installation requires approval from the local electricity distributor. In Panama, the main distributors are ENSA (Elektra Noreste S.A., subsidiary of Naturgy), EDECHI (Empresa de Distribución Eléctrica de Chiriquí), and Naturgy (formerly Gas Natural Fenosa).

E

ENSA Process

  • Submit net metering application form
  • Include single-line diagram + site plan
  • Equipment datasheets (panels + inverter)
  • ENSA technical review: 5-10 business days
  • Interconnection agreement signing
  • Post-install inspection by ENSA
N

Naturgy Process

  • Submit Solicitud de Generación Distribuida
  • Provide electrical study (signed by engineer)
  • Include transformer capacity analysis
  • Naturgy review: 10-15 business days
  • Meter installation by Naturgy
  • System commissioning and activation
STEP 3

Permits Checklist

Depending on system size and location, additional permits may be required beyond the distributor approval.

Required Permits

⚡
Electrical Permit Required for all installations. Signed by licensed electrical engineer.
🏗️
Structural Assessment Roof load analysis. Required for systems > 10kW or older buildings.
🔥
Fire Safety Fire department clearance for commercial installations > 25kW.
🌿
Environmental (ANAM) Environmental review for ground-mount systems > 100kW.
📐
Municipal Construction Local building permit if structural modifications needed.
📊
Grid Impact Study Required for systems > 100kW. Distributor may request for smaller.
TYPICAL TIMELINE

Permit Process Timeline

From initial application to system activation, the typical permit process takes 2-4 weeks for residential and 4-6 weeks for commercial.

1

Site Assessment & Design

Conduct site survey, structural assessment, electrical audit. Create system design with single-line diagram, equipment selection, and layout.

2-3 days
2

Distributor Application

Submit net metering application to ENSA/Naturgy with all technical documentation. Engineering review begins.

5-15 business days
3

Additional Permits

Submit structural, fire safety, or environmental permits in parallel with distributor review. Municipal permits if needed.

Parallel — 1-2 weeks
4

Approval & Agreement

Receive distributor approval. Sign interconnection agreement. Schedule meter installation.

2-3 days
5

Installation & Commissioning

Install system, conduct testing, schedule distributor inspection. Bidirectional meter installed. System goes live.

1-3 days install + 1-2 weeks commissioning
