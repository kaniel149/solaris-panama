1
Customer Name — Must match the person signing the solar contract. Verify cedula or RUC.
2
NIS Number — Required for ENSA net metering application. Critical for interconnection.
3
kWh Consumed — The #1 data point. Request 12 months of bills for accurate sizing.
4
Demand (kW) — Peak power draw. Important for inverter sizing and panel configuration.
5
Rate Tier — BTS-1 = Residential ($0.18/kWh), BTS-2 = Commercial ($0.195/kWh). Determines savings.
6
FET Subsidy — Fondo de Estabilización Tarifaria. Government subsidy that may be reduced/removed. Solar hedges against this risk.

FET — Fondo de Estabilización Tarifaria

  • Government subsidy that reduces electricity costs for consumers under 500 kWh/month
  • Funded by government budget — can be reduced or eliminated at any time
  • Solar systems protect clients from FET reduction risk — strong sales argument
  • Historical trend: FET has been reduced multiple times since 2019
  • When FET is reduced, clients without solar see immediate bill increases
  • Use this in proposals: "Your savings grow if electricity rates increase"
