Phase 1 — Pre-Installation

Thorough planning ensures a flawless installation day

1

Site Survey

Duration: 2-3 hours

On-site assessment to determine optimal panel placement, shading analysis, and roof conditions.

  • Roof measurements and orientation (azimuth/tilt)
  • Shading analysis using SunEye or Solar Pathfinder
  • Drone photography for complex roofs
  • GPS coordinates and compass readings
2

Structural Assessment

Duration: 1-2 hours

Verify the roof can safely support the solar array weight and wind loads for Panama's climate.

  • Roof material and age assessment
  • Load-bearing capacity calculation
  • Wind zone classification (Panama tropical)
  • Waterproofing integrity check
3

Electrical Audit

Duration: 1-2 hours

Evaluate existing electrical infrastructure for solar integration compatibility.

  • Main panel capacity and available breaker slots
  • Utility meter type (bi-directional required)
  • Grounding system evaluation
  • Wire gauge and conduit routing plan
4

System Design

Duration: 3-5 days

Engineering team creates optimized system layout using PVsyst and AutoCAD.

  • PVsyst simulation with local irradiance data
  • String sizing and inverter matching
  • Single-line electrical diagram
  • ASEP permit application preparation
  • Bill of materials finalization
🔧

Phase 2 — Day of Installation

Professional crew executes the installation with precision and safety

5

Safety Briefing

07:00 AM — 30 minutes

Full team briefing covering job hazards, emergency procedures, and PPE verification.

  • Tailboard safety meeting documentation
  • Weather assessment (no work if lightning risk)
  • Fall protection plan review
  • First aid kit and emergency contacts verified
6

Mounting Rails Installation

07:30 AM — 2-3 hours

Secure aluminum rail system to roof structure with proper flashing and sealant.

  • Lag bolt installation with flashing boots
  • Rail alignment and leveling verification
  • Torque specification: 50-70 Nm per bolt
  • Sealant application (polyurethane, UV-resistant)
7

Panel Placement

10:30 AM — 2-3 hours

Careful panel mounting with mid and end clamps, maintaining proper spacing for ventilation.

  • Module clamp torque: 18-22 Nm
  • Minimum 20mm gap between panels for ventilation
  • Panel serial number recording for warranty
  • Visual inspection for micro-cracks or damage
8

DC Wiring & String Connection

01:00 PM — 2 hours

Connect panels in series/parallel strings using MC4 connectors and route DC cables to inverter.

  • MC4 connector crimping with calibrated tool
  • String voltage verification (Voc within ±5%)
  • UV-resistant cable ties every 50cm
  • Cable management in UV-rated conduit
9

Inverter Installation

03:00 PM — 1-2 hours

Mount inverter in shaded, ventilated location and connect DC input and AC output.

  • Wall-mount with vibration dampeners
  • DC disconnect switch installation
  • AC breaker in main distribution panel
  • WiFi/Ethernet connection for monitoring
10

Grounding & Surge Protection

04:30 PM — 1 hour

Complete grounding system with equipment grounding conductors and surge protection devices.

  • Equipment grounding conductor (#6 AWG copper)
  • Ground rod installation (8ft copper-clad)
  • Type II SPD on DC and AC side
  • Ground resistance test: ≤5 ohms required
✅

Phase 3 — Post-Installation

Testing, commissioning, and official handover to client

11

System Testing

Day 2 Morning — 2 hours
  • Open circuit voltage (Voc) per string
  • Short circuit current (Isc) per string
  • Insulation resistance test (≥1MΩ)
  • Earth continuity test
  • Polarity verification all connections
12

Commissioning

Day 2 — 1 hour
  • Inverter initial startup sequence
  • Grid synchronization verification
  • Anti-islanding protection test
  • Production monitoring baseline reading
13

ASEP Inspection

Scheduled within 5-10 business days
  • ASEP inspector verifies compliance
  • Net metering agreement activation
  • Bi-directional meter installation by utility
  • Certificate of compliance issued
14

Monitoring Setup

30 minutes
  • FusionSolar / SolarEdge app installation
  • Client account creation and pairing
  • Alert thresholds configured
  • Solaris O&M team added as admin
15

Client Handover

45 minutes
  • System walkthrough and operation overview
  • Monitoring app tutorial
  • Emergency shutdown procedure training
  • Warranty documentation package
  • Maintenance schedule and contact information
QUALITY ASSURANCE

Quality Standards

Every installation meets or exceeds international quality benchmarks

IEC 62446-1 Compliance

Full documentation per IEC 62446-1: system data, inspection checklist, test results, and commissioning report delivered to every client.

Cable Management

All cables routed in UV-stabilized conduit. DC cables labeled at both ends. Drip loops at all roof penetrations. Cable ties rated for outdoor tropical use (UV + humidity).

Waterproofing Protocol

Flashing boots on all roof penetrations. Polyurethane sealant (Sikaflex or equivalent). Water test after installation with photo documentation before leaving site.

Torque Specifications

All bolted connections torqued to manufacturer specs with calibrated wrench. Lag bolts: 50-70 Nm. Mid clamps: 18-22 Nm. End clamps: 18-22 Nm. Lug terminals: per manufacturer.

SAFETY FIRST

Safety Protocols

Zero-incident commitment — every crew member goes home safe

⚠️ PPE Requirements

🪖
Hard Hat

ANSI Z89.1 Type I, Class E. Required at all times on job site.

🥽
Safety Glasses

ANSI Z87.1 impact-rated. UV protection for outdoor work.

🧤
Insulated Gloves

Class 0 rated (1000V DC). Required for all electrical connections.

👢
Safety Boots

Steel-toe, slip-resistant, EH rated. Non-marking soles for roof work.

🧗

Fall Protection

  • Full body harness required above 2m (6ft)
  • Self-retracting lifeline (SRL) anchored to structure
  • Roof edge warning line at 2m from edge
  • Ladder secured at top and bottom
⚡

Electrical Lockout/Tagout

  • LOTO procedure before any electrical work
  • DC isolator OFF before string connections
  • Panels produce voltage in daylight — cover if needed
  • Multimeter verification before touching conductors
🌧️

Weather Protocols

  • Stop work if lightning within 10 miles
  • Wait 30 minutes after last thunder/lightning
  • Wet roof = no roof work (slip hazard)
  • Wind gusts >40 km/h = suspend panel lifting
