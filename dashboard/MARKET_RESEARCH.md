## 3. Ley 417 — Tax Incentives (Dec 2023)

### Benefits
- **100% import duty exemption** — panels, inverters, batteries, mounting, cables
- **100% ITBMS exemption** (7% VAT equivalent) — equipment
- **5% selective consumption tax eliminated** — new in Ley 417
- **All other fees/taxes exonerated** — aranceles, contributions

### Requirements
- ASEP license (for grid-connected public generation)
- Secretaría Nacional de Energía certification (for self-consumption)
- Professional electrical/structural plans
- Bidirectional meter + ENSA/Naturgy approval for net metering
- Self-consumption limit: up to 500 kW via net metering
- Processing cost: $600-1,200 depending on province/scale

### Impact
- Makes solar equipment ~12-15% cheaper (duty + tax savings)
- Full investment deductible from income tax
- Applies to residential, commercial, and generation projects

## 8. Regulatory Environment

### ASEP (Autoridad Nacional de los Servicios Públicos)
- Regulates all electricity generation and distribution
- Issues installer licenses
- Sets technical standards for grid connection

### Net Metering
- Up to 500 kW self-consumption
- Bidirectional meter required
- Excess credited on next bill (not paid out)
- Process: apply to ENSA/Naturgy with system design + structural cert

### Distributors
- **ENSA** (Naturgy subsidiary) — Panama City, Azuero, most of country
- **EDEMET** — Western Panama City metro
- **EDECHI** — Chiriquí, Bocas del Toro
