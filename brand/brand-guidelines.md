## 5. Photography Style

### Aesthetic
- **Natural light preferred.** Golden hour is our sweet spot.
- **Tropical but not touristy.** Show the real Panama ‚Äî lush, warm, authentic.
- **People in context.** Show homeowners on their roofs, business owners in their properties, installers at work.
- **Architecture matters.** Frame solar panels as a design element, not an eyesore.

### Subject Guidelines
- Solar panels on beautiful buildings (beach houses, boutique hotels, modern homes)
- The Azuero coastline and landscape
- Installation crews at work (professional, safety-focused)
- Happy clients in their properties
- Close-up details: panels, inverters, clean wiring

### Color Treatment
- Warm color temperature. Slight golden cast.
- High contrast, not flat. Shadows should be rich, not crushed.
- Never over-saturated. Natural palette.

### Don'ts
- ‚úó No stock photos of generic solar farms in deserts
- ‚úó No cheesy thumbs-up or handshake photos
- ‚úó No cold/blue color grading
- ‚úó No drone shots that make panels look like an afterthought

## 7. Brand Applications ‚Äî Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- ‚úì Use generous whitespace ‚Äî let the design breathe
- ‚úì Pair Solaris Green with Solar Gold sparingly (gold is the spice, not the dish)
- ‚úì Use photography with warm, golden tones
- ‚úì Maintain consistent 8px spacing grid
- ‚úì Round corners at 8px for cards, 4px for buttons, full-round for badges
- ‚úì Use subtle shadows (0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.06))
- ‚úì Reference Law 417 (Incentivos para Energ√≠a Solar) in commercial materials

### Don't
- ‚úó Use more than 3 colors in a single layout
- ‚úó Center-align body text
- ‚úó Use gradients (exception: subtle background transitions on hero sections)
- ‚úó Use clip art or emoji as design elements
- ‚úó Mix brand fonts with system fonts
- ‚úó Use pure black (#000000) ‚Äî always use Charcoal (#1A1A1A)
- ‚úó Use pure white (#FFFFFF) ‚Äî use Pearl White (#FEFDFB)
- ‚úó Overuse gold ‚Äî it's an accent, not a primary
