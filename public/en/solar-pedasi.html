Why Solar Here

Why Pedasí was made for solar

Pedasí sits in Panama's dry arc — one of the sunniest corners of Central America. That's exactly the climate solar panels were built for.

5+ Sun-Hours, Every Day

Pedasí averages more than five peak sun-hours per day, with solar irradiance around 5.2 kWh/m². Production stays strong year-round — even in the green season, mornings are mostly clear and your panels keep working.

Beat $0.26/kWh Utility Rates

Electricity here is billed in USD by the utility (Naturgy/ENSA), and residential rates hover around $0.26 per kWh — among the highest in the region. A home running air conditioning can easily see $250–$600 monthly bills. Solar typically cuts that by up to 95%.

Quieter Than a Generator

Many expats price out a diesel generator for backup. Generators are loud, need fuel runs and constant maintenance, and cost far more per kWh than solar. A solar system — with optional battery backup — is silent, clean, and pays for itself.

Tax Incentives

Law 417: How Panama's solar incentives work for foreigners

Law 417 is Panama's clean-energy incentive law — and yes, it works for foreigners. The benefits attach to the property or business owner in Panama, not to your passport.

What Law 417 means for a Pedasí homeowner

  • Lower upfront price for everyone: solar panels, inverters and components enter Panama exempt from import duties and the 7% ITBMS sales tax
  • Income tax deduction: if you or your Panamanian corporation earn Panama-source income (rentals, a hotel, a restaurant), the investment can be deducted from income tax (ISR)
  • Applies to residents and non-resident property owners alike — the benefit follows the property or business, not your visa status
  • Excess energy you export earns credits on your utility bill under net metering
  • Solaris manages all Law 417 filings and permits at no extra cost

To be honest about it: the import and ITBMS exemptions benefit every buyer because they lower the system price. The income-tax deduction only helps if you (or your Panama corporation) actually pay income tax in Panama. Non-resident owners with no Panama-source income still come out ahead on equipment cost, net metering and property value — and we'll tell you exactly which benefits apply to your situation before you sign anything.

Net Metering

Net metering, explained simply

Panama's net metering program (medición neta) lets your system push extra energy into the grid during the day and pull it back at night. A bidirectional meter tracks both flows, and you're billed only on the difference. Produce more than you use in a month, and the surplus becomes a credit on your next bill.

There's no complicated energy trading and no need to oversize your system. We design for your real consumption, and we file the interconnection application with the utility on your behalf — you don't touch the paperwork.

FAQ

Questions expats ask us in Pedasí

Can foreigners benefit from Law 417?

Yes. Law 417's benefits apply to the property or business owner in Panama, regardless of nationality. The import-duty and ITBMS exemptions lower the equipment price for every buyer; the income tax deduction applies if you or your Panamanian corporation have Panama-source income, such as rentals or a local business.

Do you speak English?

Yes — our team serves English-speaking clients across Azuero. Site visits, quotes, contracts and after-sales support are available in English and Spanish.

How much does a solar system cost in Pedasí?

Most residential systems (5–10 kWp) run between $8,000 and $18,000 installed, depending on roof type and whether you add battery backup. At current utility rates, typical payback is 3–5 years.

Does solar work during power outages?

A standard grid-tied system switches off during an outage for safety reasons. Add a battery and your essential circuits — fridge, lights, internet, a bedroom AC — keep running automatically. It's a popular option on the Pedasí coast.

How long does the whole process take?

From signed quote to a system that's switched on, usually 2–4 weeks. Installation itself takes 1–3 days; the rest is utility interconnection paperwork, which we handle for you.
