Tax Incentives

Law 417 works for foreigners too

Panama's clean-energy law rewards the owner of the property or business — residents and non-resident owners alike. Your passport is irrelevant.

What you get under Law 417

  • Solar equipment enters Panama free of import duties and the 7% ITBMS sales tax — lowering the price for every buyer
  • The investment is deductible from Panamanian income tax (ISR) if you or your Panamanian corporation have Panama-source income
  • Benefits follow the property or business — they apply whether you live here full-time or own from abroad
  • Net metering credits for every surplus kilowatt-hour you send to the grid
  • Solaris prepares and files all the paperwork at no extra cost

The honest version: equipment exemptions help everyone; the income-tax deduction only has value if you actually pay income tax in Panama (rental income, a hotel, a local business). If you don't, you still win on system price, net metering and property value — and we'll tell you exactly where you stand before you spend a dollar.

Net Metering

Net metering in plain English

Panama's net metering program (medición neta) turns the grid into your battery. A bidirectional meter counts energy in both directions: your surplus flows out during the day, you draw back at night, and you're billed only on the difference. Overproduce in a month, and the credit rolls onto your next bill.

We size the system to your consumption, and we handle the entire interconnection process with the utility — application, inspection, meter swap. You sign, we file.

FAQ

Questions expats ask us

Can foreigners benefit from Law 417?

Yes. Law 417's benefits apply to the property or business owner in Panama, regardless of nationality. Import-duty and ITBMS exemptions lower the equipment price for every buyer; the income tax deduction applies if you or your Panamanian corporation have Panama-source income. We'll explain exactly which benefits apply to your situation.

Do you speak English?

Yes — our team serves English-speaking clients across Azuero. Site visits, quotes, contracts and after-sales support are all available in English and Spanish.

How much does solar cost in Panama?

Typical residential systems (5–10 kWp) run $8,000–$18,000 installed, in USD. Commercial systems are quoted per project. With utility rates around $0.26/kWh, payback is usually 3–5 years.

Which areas do you serve?

We install across the Azuero Peninsula: Pedasí, Playa Venao, Las Tablas, Chitré, Tonosí, Cañas and surrounding communities in Los Santos and Herrera.

Do I need batteries, or is grid-tied enough?

Under net metering, a grid-tied system alone slashes your bill — the grid effectively stores your midday surplus. Batteries are worth adding if you want backup power during outages, which are more common toward the coast.
