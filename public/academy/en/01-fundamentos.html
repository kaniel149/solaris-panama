Module 1: Solar Fundamentals

Solaris Panama Sales Academy

Who this module is for: New salespeople with no prior solar energy experience.
Estimated time: 2–3 hours of reading + role-play exercise.

🎯 Module Objectives

By the end of this module you will be able to:

  1. Explain in simple terms how a solar system works.
  2. Read a Panamanian electricity bill and identify the key data points (kWh, rate, fixed charge).
  3. Explain Law 417 to a client without using legal language.
  4. Calculate (at a basic level) the return on investment (ROI) of a solar system.
  5. Answer with confidence why Azuero is one of the best solar markets in the world.

Part 1: How Solar Energy Works

The idea in one sentence

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity. That electricity powers your business. What you don't use, you sell back to the grid. What the grid would have charged you, you now produce for free on your own rooftop.

The process step by step

```

SUN → PANELS → INVERTER → BUILDING → GRID (surplus)

```

  1. The panels capture light. Each panel is made of silicon cells that react to sunlight and generate direct current (DC) electricity.
  2. The inverter converts the current. The inverter transforms DC current into alternating current (AC), the same type used by every outlet in your business.
  3. The building consumes first. Solar electricity powers your equipment in real time. If you produce more than you consume at that moment, the surplus flows to the grid.
  4. The bidirectional meter records everything. With net metering, the meter tracks what you deliver to the grid and deducts it from your next bill.

What happens at night or when it rains?

At night the panels don't generate. The business continues drawing power from the grid, as always. The difference is that credits accumulated during the day reduce the net cost. In a well-sized system, the bill can drop 60–80%.

Important: panels need light, not heat

A cloudy day still generates between 10–30% of maximum capacity. In Azuero, even the rainy months have usable sunshine hours in the morning.

Part 2: The Panamanian Electricity Bill

The three data points to ask every prospect for

Data pointWhere it appears on the billWhy we need it
Monthly consumption (kWh)"Consumo del período"To size the system
Total amount ($)"Total a pagar"To calculate savings and ROI
Rate ($/kWh)"Precio por kWh" or by dividing amount ÷ kWhTo compare with our offer

Commercial reference rate in Panama

The average commercial rate in Panama is approximately $0.20 per kWh. That is the number we use in our savings calculations.

Note: Electricity rates in Panama have risen more than 30% over the last 5 years. Every year the client waits, their bill grows and our proposal becomes more attractive.

Quick bill-reading exercise

If a hotel shows you a bill of $4,500 with 22,500 kWh consumed:

  • Rate = $4,500 ÷ 22,500 = $0.20/kWh ✓
  • Recommended system: depends on the roof, but with 22,500 kWh/month we need to assess the available area.
  • The manager will calculate the exact system — your job is to get the bill.

Part 3: Law 417 Explained Simply

What is Law 417?

It is a Panamanian government law that incentivizes the use of solar energy. Think of it as the government telling the client: "If you install solar, we'll help you pay less."

The four main benefits

BenefitWhat it means in plain language
Tax exemption on equipment and installationThe client doesn't pay certain taxes on the cost of the system
Duty-free importationPanels and inverters enter the country without paying customs duties, which lowers the cost of the system
Net meteringThe client can "sell" surplus energy back to the grid and deduct it from their bill
Applicable up to 500 kWAny commercial building in Azuero falls within the limit — no size restriction for our clients

Why mention Law 417 on every visit?

Because it shortens the payback period by 12 to 18 months. A system that would normally pay for itself in 4 years can, with the Law 417 benefits, pay back in 2.5 to 3.5 years. That shifts the conversation from "big investment" to "fast investment."

How to talk about urgency honestly

"The benefits of Law 417 exist today. Policies can change. I'm not telling you to decide today, but I am telling you that every month you wait is a month without savings — and with the risk that those incentives get reduced."

Part 4: The ROI Concept

ROI = When do I get my money back?

ROI (Return on Investment) in solar means: how many years it takes for the electricity savings to pay for the cost of the system. After that point, electricity is essentially free for the next 20+ years.

A concrete example to memorize

ConceptNumber
Installed system (100 kW, mid-size hotel)$120,000
Monthly electricity savings$3,200
Annual savings$38,400
Payback period~3.1 years
Savings over 25 years$840,000+
Phrase to use with clients: "In 3 years, the system has already paid for itself. The next 22 years are pure gain."

What you do NOT calculate

You collect the data (bill, roof, consumption). The manager and the technical team calculate the exact ROI and present it in the formal proposal. Never give ROI numbers from memory without the manager's backing.

Part 5: Why Azuero and Panama Are an Extraordinary Solar Market

The sun

Panama receives 5 to 6 kWh/m² of solar radiation per day. That number is world-class. For comparison, Germany — the country with the most solar installations per capita in the world — receives just 3 kWh/m²/day. Azuero is among the sunniest areas in all of Central America.

The market

  • Solaris Panama is the only company focused on commercial rooftop solar in Azuero. There is no direct competition. Every conversation is a fresh opportunity, not a battle against another provider.
  • Ecotourism in the region grows 20% per year. Hotels that install solar have a real marketing advantage over environmentally conscious travelers.
  • Power outages are frequent in rural areas. Solar + net metering provides partial energy independence — a compelling argument for farms and hotels.

The takeaway for the salesperson

When you walk into a hotel, a supermarket, or a farm, you are not selling something nobody has heard of. You are selling the most obvious solution to the most expensive problem that business has: its electricity bill.

📚 Solar Vocabulary (Glossary)

Learn these terms. Clients will use them or you will need them to communicate with the technical team.

TermSimple definition
kW (kilowatt)Unit of power. Measures how much energy something produces or consumes at a given moment. A 100 kW system can produce 100,000 watts under ideal conditions.
kWh (kilowatt-hour)Unit of energy. This is what appears on the bill. 1 kWh = using 1,000 watts for 1 hour. The grid price is ~$0.20 per kWh.
kWp (kilowatt-peak)The maximum capacity of a panel or system under ideal laboratory conditions. In real life, slightly less is produced.
Solar panel / photovoltaic moduleThe blue or black plate that goes on the roof. Converts sunlight into electricity.
InverterDevice that converts DC current from the panels into AC current used by the building. It is the "brain" of the system.
DC current (direct current)The type of electricity produced by the panels. Flows in one direction.
AC current (alternating current)The type of electricity used by outlets and equipment. The inverter converts DC to AC.
Net meteringSystem where the meter tracks what you deliver to the grid and deducts it from your bill. Enabled by Law 417.
Law 417Panamanian law that exempts solar equipment from taxes and allows commercial net metering up to 500 kW.
ASEPNational Authority for Public Services. The government body that approves net metering applications in Panama. Solaris handles those applications.
Payback / Payback periodThe time (in years) it takes for electricity savings to pay for the cost of the system. At Solaris: 2.5 to 4.5 years depending on the option chosen.
ROI (Return on Investment)In solar terms: how much money the client saves over 25 years compared to what they invested.
On-grid systemSystem connected to the electrical grid. This is what Solaris installs. Allows net metering.
Racking / StructureThe metal frame that holds the panels on the roof.
Panel warranty (25 years)Manufacturers guarantee that panels will produce at least 80% of their original capacity for 25 years.
System sizingThe process of calculating how many kW of system a client needs, based on their consumption and roof size. Performed by the technical team.
Solar billThe client's electricity bill after installing solar. Can be $0 or very low if the system is well-sized.
Solar irradiationThe amount of solar energy reaching one square meter per day. In Azuero: 5–6 kWh/m²/day.
Turnkey systemSolaris delivers everything: design, permits, installation and grid connection. The client manages nothing.
Escalator (in PPA)The annual percentage increase in the PPA rate. At Solaris: 1–2%/year, versus 3–5%/year for the grid.

📞 Scripts and Key Phrases

To explain how solar works in 30 seconds

"Let me explain how it works in simple terms. The panels on your roof capture the sun and convert that energy into electricity. That electricity powers your hotel [business] directly. Whatever you don't use, the grid receives and credits to your next bill. At the end of the month, you only pay the difference — which can be up to 70–80% less."

To explain Law 417 without technical jargon

"The Panamanian government created Law 417 to encourage the use of solar energy. What that means for you specifically: first, you don't pay import taxes on the equipment, which lowers the cost of the system. Second, you can sell back to the grid the energy you produce but don't consume. Third, it applies to systems up to 500 kW, so you qualify without any issue."

To introduce the ROI concept

"What we're going to calculate together is when the system pays for itself. On projects like yours, it's typically between 2.5 and 4 years. After that, the electricity you produce is essentially free for the next 20 years. That's what we call the return on investment."

To handle "the sun here isn't enough"

"That is exactly the opposite of what the data shows. Azuero receives between 5 and 6 kWh of solar radiation per square meter per day. Germany — the country with the most solar installations per capita in the world — receives less than 3. You have twice the sun they do."

⚠️ Common Mistakes in This Module

  1. Giving ROI numbers without backing. Never tell a client "your payback would be X years" without the manager having reviewed the calculation. Always use ranges: "between 2.5 and 4.5 years, depending on the system and the tax benefits."
  1. Confusing kW with kWh. kW is power (capacity at a given moment), kWh is energy (accumulated consumption). The bill uses kWh. The system is measured in kW.
  1. Saying solar doesn't work when it rains. It works at lower efficiency, but it still generates. In Azuero, even during the rainy season there are sufficient sunshine hours.
  1. Not asking for the electricity bill. Without that data, the technical team cannot size the system. Always leave the first visit with a photo of the bill or the kWh number and the monthly amount.
  1. Inventing Law 417 benefits. Only mention the four that are in this module. If the client asks something you don't know, say: "Let me confirm that with the manager and I'll get back to you today."
